## Email 2: Quick Win — RPM Basics (Day 2)

**Subject:** The number that determines your TikTok payout
**Preview text:** It's not your total view count. Here's what actually matters.

---

Hi [first_name],

Your Creator Rewards payout is based on RPM — revenue per mille, meaning revenue per 1,000 qualified views.

Two important words there: qualified views. Not total views. Not follower count. Qualified views.

A qualified view requires your video to be at least 1 minute long, your viewer to watch a meaningful portion of it, and the view to come from an eligible region. Views from bots, very-short-watch-time traffic, and ineligible regions don't count.

So if your total view count is high but your Creator Rewards earnings are low, the first question to ask is: how many of those views are actually qualified?

The RPM calculator on the site runs the math for you:
→ [Link to RPM calculator]

Put in your qualified view count (found in Creator Studio → Creator Rewards analytics), not your total view count. The number it gives you is an estimate, but it's a grounded one.

— [Site name / author name]

P.S. RPM varies by niche and audience geography. If a significant portion of your audience is in regions with lower advertising rates, your RPM will be lower than average — that's not something wrong with your account, it's just the economics of where your viewers are.

## Email 3: Common Mistake — Qualified Views (Day 5)

**Subject:** Why your views might not be counting
**Preview text:** Three specific reasons Creator Rewards isn't counting the views you think it should.

---

Hi [first_name],

The most common frustration I hear from creators in the Creator Rewards Program: "I have X thousand views, why are my earnings so low?"

Almost always, it comes down to qualified views being much lower than total views. Here are three reasons that happens:

**1. Video length below 60 seconds.** Any video under 1 minute generates zero qualified views, regardless of engagement. If you have a mix of short and long videos in your recent history, the short ones pull your qualified view count down without pulling your total view count down.

**2. High drop-off rate.** TikTok's qualified view calculation considers watch time. If viewers consistently leave after 5–10 seconds, fewer of those views meet the threshold — even if the video is over 60 seconds.

**3. Audience geography.** Views from regions outside TikTok's eligible list don't count as qualified. If a video goes viral in a region TikTok doesn't support for Creator Rewards, those views look impressive in your analytics but don't generate earnings.

The fix in each case is different. The guide below covers all three with specific steps:

P.S. If you're finding that most of your content is under 60 seconds and you want to stay in Creator Rewards, the shift to longer content doesn't have to be a total format change. Some creators keep their short-form content and add a dedicated series of longer videos. The longer videos carry the CRP earnings; the short videos carry the reach.
