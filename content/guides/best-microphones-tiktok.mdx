## Match Your Mic to Your Content Type

Before buying, ask yourself how you actually film:

- **Moving/outdoor content** (travel, street, fitness) needs a wireless lavalier. Phone mics can't handle wind, distance, or crowd noise.
- **Talking-head/tutorial content** filmed solo works well with a wireless lav or a wired shotgun plugged into your phone.
- **TikTok LIVE from a desk** needs a USB mic. It's hands-free, consistent, and signals a professional setup to your audience.
- **Collab/interview content** needs a dual-channel wireless system.
- **ASMR** has specific condenser mic requirements not covered in this guide.

## What to Look for When Buying

**Wireless vs. wired.** Wireless lavaliers are worth the price for any creator who moves while filming. If you film sitting at a desk, wired USB is simpler.

**32-bit float.** Only the DJI Mic 2 in this guide has 32-bit float. It prevents audio clipping at loud volumes. If you make high-energy, loud content, it matters. For normal talking-head or tutorial content, it's a nice-to-have.

**Connectors.** Check what connector your phone has before ordering. iPhone Lightning, iPhone USB-C (iPhone 15+), and Android USB-C are all different. The Hollyland Lark M2 includes all three adapters, which is why it's the easiest recommendation.

**On-board recording backup.** Both the DJI Mic 2 and RODE Wireless GO II record internally as a backup. That's real insurance for outdoor content where wireless signals can drop.

**Budget threshold.** If you're under $100 and need wireless, get the Hollyland Lark M2S or Lark M2. If budget stretches to $150+, the DJI Mic 2 is the upgrade that makes sense.

## FAQ

**Do I need a microphone for TikTok?**

For lip-sync and dance content, no. For talking-head, tutorial, outdoor, or LIVE content, a mic upgrade will make a real difference. TikTok's audio compression is hard on phone mic audio, and cleaner audio keeps people watching.

**What's the best wireless mic for TikTok under $100?**

The Hollyland Lark M2 (2TX) or Lark M2S (1TX). Both deliver broadcast-quality audio at under $100 with all connectors included.

**Should I get the DJI Mic 2 or the RODE Wireless GO II?**

For solo TikTok creators, the DJI Mic 2 wins on specs (32-bit float, magnetic clip, slightly better range). The Rode wins if you prefer the Rode ecosystem or need to integrate with Rode Connect software.

**What microphone do I need for TikTok LIVE?**

A USB desk mic. The Blue Yeti is the standard recommendation. It signals a professional setup and works across multiple scenarios (solo talking-head, interview guests, group LIVE).
