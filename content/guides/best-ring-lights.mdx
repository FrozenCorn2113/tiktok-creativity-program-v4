## What Actually Matters for TikTok (vs. What Doesn't)

Before the product breakdown, the context. Here's what the spec sheet gets wrong.

**CRI matters less for most creators than you'd think.** CRI measures color accuracy. It matters for beauty tutorials where a brand's product color needs to read correctly on camera. For talking-head or lifestyle content, a CRI of 90+ is nice but not the decision point.

**Size matters more than specs.** A 10-inch ring light puts out noticeably less light than an 18-inch. If you're shooting in a bright room, a 10-inch at full power can work fine. If you shoot in a bedroom with no natural light, you might need the 18-inch even though the spec sheets look similar on paper.

**The phone holder is what actually fails.** Budget ring lights frequently have weak clip pressure. It holds a phone at landscape angle but can't hold a phone securely at portrait (9:16) orientation. At portrait, the phone is top-heavy and a weak clip will drift or drop. Test this before you film twenty videos and wonder why your phone shifted.

**USB power is convenient; battery power is a different product.** USB-powered ring lights require an outlet or USB battery bank. Battery-powered options cost significantly more and are only worth it if you film away from a fixed location.

## What Size Ring Light Do You Actually Need?

The question that drives the most confusion: 10-inch vs 12-inch vs 18-inch.

**10–12 inch:** Works for creators who film seated at a desk, close to the camera. The light is closer to your face, which compensates for the smaller diameter. Good for talking-head content in a fixed, close-up setup.

**18-inch:** Better for creators who film standing, or who need softer, more diffused light for beauty content. The larger diameter means better coverage at a wider distance. If you're not sure, the 18-inch is the safer choice.

The tripod height matters as much as the ring size. Check that spec before buying.
