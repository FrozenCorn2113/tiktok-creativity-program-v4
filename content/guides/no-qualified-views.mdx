# Why You Have No Qualified Views on TikTok (And How to Fix It)

Your video has 100,000 views. Your Creator Rewards dashboard shows $0. You're not alone, and it's probably not a bug.

This happens to creators every day, usually on a video they were counting on. The frustrating part is that TikTok's qualified view criteria are strict and non-obvious. Most people discover them after the fact, when the damage is already done.

This guide explains every reason a view fails to qualify, walks through a 7-step diagnostic to find your specific problem, and covers what to do about it.

<CalloutBox type="info" title="What counts as a qualified view">
  <ul>
    <li>Video must be over 1 minute long. Sub-60 second videos earn nothing, for any view.</li>
    <li>The view must come from the For You Page (FYP). Profile visits, Following feed, and direct shares don't count.</li>
    <li>Viewer must watch at least 5 seconds (FYP traffic) or 30 seconds (Search traffic).</li>
    <li>No earnings until a video reaches 1,000 qualified views.</li>
  </ul>
</CalloutBox>

## Every reason a view doesn't count

A view fails to qualify if any single condition below is true. You don't need multiple problems. One is enough to drop that view to zero.

| Reason | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Video is under 60 seconds | The entire video is ineligible. Not individual views — the whole video. No fix except reuploading as a longer version. |
| View came from a non-qualifying source | Profile visits, Following feed, direct share links, embedded players, and external URLs generate no qualified views. FYP and search views both qualify as of December 2025. |
| Viewer watched less than 5 seconds (FYP) | For FYP traffic, 5 seconds is the floor. Anything shorter is counted as an impression, not a qualified view. |
| Viewer watched less than 5 seconds (Search) | As of December 2025, search views qualify with the same 5-second threshold as FYP views. |
| Viewer tapped "Not Interested" | Removes the view from qualification immediately and tells TikTok to reduce distribution. |
| View from TikTok Promote | Promoted views never count. Using Promote on a video adds non-qualifying views and doesn't move your earnings. |
| Duplicate viewer | Only the first view from a unique account counts. Multiple watches from the same viewer only earn once. |
| Below 1,000 qualified view threshold | TikTok pays nothing until a video reaches 1,000 qualified views. 999 qualified views earns zero. |
| Video posted before joining the program | Videos posted before a creator joined Creator Rewards don't qualify, regardless of performance. |
| Account is a Business account | Business accounts cannot participate in Creator Rewards. Qualified views may show in analytics, but no payment is made. |
| Account has standing violations | Active community guideline strikes can suspend qualified view earnings, partially or fully. |
| Viewer is in an ineligible country | Views from outside the 8 eligible markets don't count even if all other criteria are met. Eligible: US, UK, Germany, France, Brazil, Mexico, Japan, South Korea. |
| Artificial or bot views | TikTok filters these automatically. Accounts that have purchased views may see qualified view counts permanently suppressed. |
| Duet or Stitch | These formats are categorically ineligible for Creator Rewards, regardless of length or performance. |

## The "500K views, $2 earnings" pattern explained

This is the most common scenario creators search for. It feels like a bug. It almost never is.

Here's the math that explains it:

500,000 total views x 2% FYP qualification rate = 10,000 qualified views x $0.50 RPM = $5.00 earned.

That 2% figure isn't random. It reflects a video where most traffic came from profile visits and Following feed, plus a large portion of FYP viewers scrolled past before the 5-second mark. The total view count looks impressive. The qualified view count is tiny.

The diagnosis usually has two or three contributing factors: most traffic came from non-FYP sources, FYP viewers had poor retention, and the video may have gone viral in countries outside the eligible 8 markets. Each factor compounds the others. A video that fixes only one of them earns more, but not proportionally. All three need to improve.

## The 7-step diagnostic

Work through these in order. The first step that returns a "yes" is your problem. Don't skip ahead. Most creators find their answer in steps 1–3.

**Step 1: Check video length.**

Is the video over 60 seconds? If no, this video is permanently ineligible. No amount of optimization changes that. The only path is reuploading a version that runs longer than 60 seconds.

**Step 2: Check traffic sources in TikTok Studio.**

Go to TikTok Studio > Video Analytics > Traffic Source. What percentage of views came from "For You" (the FYP)?

A healthy video for Creator Rewards purposes has 40%+ FYP traffic. Below 20% is a red flag. If most of your traffic is from Profile, Following, or direct links, that's your primary cause and also the most fixable.

Search traffic now qualifies under the same rules as FYP traffic (5-second minimum, as of December 2025). This makes search optimization a direct earnings lever, not just a growth tactic.

**Step 3: Check average watch time.**

TikTok Studio > Video Analytics > Average Watch Time. Is your average above 5 seconds?

If your average watch time is hovering near or below 5 seconds, the hook is failing. Most of your FYP viewers are scrolling past before the qualified threshold, which means even strong FYP delivery doesn't convert to qualified views.

**Step 4: Check the Creator Rewards Dashboard.**

TikTok Studio > Creator Rewards > Video Performance. Look at the ratio of Qualified Views to Total Views for this specific video.

A ratio below 10% points to a structural problem: traffic source or watch time. A ratio of 0% on a video that's over 60 seconds means something more specific is blocking it. Move to step 5.

**Step 5: Check account status.**

The Creator Rewards dashboard displays a notice if the account has been suspended from the program. Also check: is this a personal account, not a Business account? Are there any active community guideline strikes? Was this video posted before you joined the program?

Any of these will produce zero earnings even when all other signals look fine.

**Step 6: Check the video's eligibility.**

Is it a Duet or Stitch? Ineligible. Does it contain significant reposted or watermarked content from another platform? Likely ineligible. Was it boosted with TikTok Promote? Promoted views won't count.

**Step 7: Rule out dashboard lag.**

The Creator Rewards dashboard updates daily, but estimated earnings can take 1–3 days to appear after views are counted. If the video was posted less than 72 hours ago, wait before concluding there's a problem.

If it's been 4 or more days and qualified views are still showing zero on a video that passed all the checks above, that's when you start looking at platform-side bugs.

## Reading your analytics in TikTok Studio

The data you need is all in TikTok Studio. Here's where to find each metric and what it tells you.

| Metric | Where to Find | What It Tells You |
|---|---|---|
| Traffic source breakdown | Video Analytics > Traffic Source | FYP % vs Profile % vs Search % vs Following % |
| Average watch time | Video Analytics > Average Watch Time | Whether viewers are clearing the 5-second threshold |
| Qualified views | Creator Rewards > Video Performance | How many views actually counted |
| Qualified view rate | Qualified Views divided by Total Views | Your qualification percentage for this video |
| Earnings estimate | Creator Rewards Dashboard | Estimated payout (1–3 day delay after views are counted) |

On traffic source: "For You" / FYP views and "Search" views both qualify (same 5-second threshold, as of December 2025). "Profile visits" and "Following" never qualify. "Sound," "Hashtag," and "Trending" route back through FYP and can qualify.

If traffic source data isn't showing, the account may need to switch to Creator mode. Personal accounts don't get full analytics. Business accounts don't participate in Creator Rewards at all. If you're on a Business account, that's the answer.

## How to shift traffic toward FYP

If your diagnostic found a traffic source problem (most views coming from Profile or Following), here's how to fix it.

FYP traffic comes from TikTok testing your content on new audiences. When the algorithm sees strong early retention in that test batch, it expands distribution to more FYP users. When early retention is weak, the video gets pushed to existing followers instead, which means Following feed traffic and non-qualifying views.

**The hook is the FYP signal.** TikTok tests each video on a small batch of FYP users. If first-3-second retention is strong, distribution expands. If it's weak, the video exits FYP and reaches mostly your existing followers. Every view after that goes to Following feed, not FYP.

**Keyword-optimized content attracts FYP and search simultaneously.** Trend-chasing content tends to pull profile and following traffic from existing fans looking for your take. Content built around search demand pulls new audiences who've never seen your account. Those are FYP and search views, which qualify.

**Longer videos with narrative payoffs get more FYP push.** TikTok is more likely to expand FYP distribution for longer content when early retention is strong. A video that hooks 80% of the first test batch past 15 seconds gets broader FYP exposure than one that loses half its viewers in 5 seconds.

**Use Creator Search Insights.** In TikTok Studio, go to Inspiration > Creator Search Insights. This shows you what people are actually searching for in your niche. Topics with high search demand and low content supply tend to get FYP distribution plus search traffic at the same time.

Watch the first 100–500 views closely. If that early FYP batch shows less than 30% watch-through, TikTok pulls FYP distribution almost immediately. The video reaches only your existing followers from that point forward, and those views go to Following feed, not FYP.

## Dashboard lag and known bugs

Most "my earnings aren't updating" problems are not bugs. They are creators checking too early.

The dashboard updates daily. Estimated earnings take 1–3 days to appear after views are counted. Final earnings for a given month settle on the 1st of the following month. If your video was posted less than 72 hours ago, wait.

Creators do report real display bugs: qualified view counts that freeze for several days then jump, "watch time 0" showing on videos with thousands of views, dashboards that stop updating during platform updates. These are real. They're also not the explanation most of the time. Rule out disqualification first.

If it's been 4 or more days, the video meets all the criteria above, and the dashboard still shows zero:

- Refresh on both mobile and desktop.
- Log out and back in to force a data sync.
- Check creator community forums for reports of platform-wide issues.
- Contact TikTok Creator Support through TikTok Studio. This option is available for accounts enrolled in the Creator Rewards Program.

Do not interpret a 24-hour zero as a problem. The most common "bug" is a creator checking earnings before the first update cycle runs.

## Account-level blockers

**Business account.** Business accounts are ineligible for Creator Rewards. The fix is converting to a Personal (Creator) account. Note that some Business account features are lost on conversion. Even with the conversion complete, videos posted while the account was a Business account don't retroactively qualify.

**Standing violations.** Community guidelines violations can partially or permanently remove a creator from the program. Five total account violations result in permanent ineligibility. Active temporary bans also suspend earnings for their duration. Creators who believe they were wrongly removed can appeal within 30 days of receiving the notification through the in-app notification path.

If an account was removed and you've seen third-party services offering reinstatement, skip them. The appeal through TikTok's notification is the only legitimate path.

**Age and account type.** Account holder must be 18 or older with verified age for payment. Government and political organization accounts are ineligible regardless of account type or follower count.
