# TikTok Scaled LIVE Rewards: Complete Guide to Earning Up to 53%

*Last verified: March 2026. Confirm current requirements at TikTok's Creator Center before relying on specific numbers.*

TikTok launched Scaled LIVE Rewards on February 10, 2026, and it changed the math for live streamers. Before this, LIVE monetization meant waiting for gift donations from viewers. Now there's a structured revenue share that rewards consistency and audience growth, not just generosity from chat.

The headline number: up to 53% of your LIVE rewards revenue. That breaks down into 40% from Per-LIVE payouts and 13% from Weekly Missions. Getting to 53% takes effort, but the structure is transparent, which is more than most creator programs offer.

---

## What Scaled LIVE Rewards Actually Is

Scaled LIVE Rewards sits on top of TikTok's existing LIVE gifting system. It doesn't replace gifts. Instead, it adds a performance-based revenue layer that pays you based on how long you stream, how often you stream, and how many new followers you pick up during sessions.

Think of it as two separate income tracks running at the same time:

1. **Per-LIVE Payouts (up to 40%)** pay out after each qualifying session
2. **Weekly Missions (up to 13%)** pay out when you hit weekly targets

Both tracks reward the same behaviors: longer streams, consistent scheduling, and growing your audience during lives. The difference is timing and structure.

---

## Per-LIVE Payouts: The 40% Track

Every time you finish a qualifying LIVE session, you earn a percentage of the rewards generated during that stream. The base rate starts below 40% and scales up based on session performance.

### What counts as a qualifying session

Your LIVE must run for a **minimum of 25 minutes** to qualify for Per-LIVE payouts. Sessions shorter than 25 minutes earn nothing through this track (though viewers can still send gifts as normal).

The 25-minute minimum exists because TikTok wants creators who build real viewing sessions, not people who pop in for five minutes hoping for quick donations.

### How the percentage scales

The Per-LIVE payout percentage increases based on:

- **Session duration** beyond 25 minutes (longer sessions earn a higher cut)
- **Viewer engagement** during the stream (comments, shares, new follows)
- **Follower acquisition** during the session (new followers gained while live)

The exact formula TikTok uses to calculate where you fall within the 0-40% range isn't public. What creators have reported is that sessions running 45-60+ minutes with active chat and follower growth consistently hit the upper end of the range. ## Weekly Missions: The 13% Track

Weekly Missions work like a bonus layer. Each week, TikTok assigns a set of missions tied to your LIVE activity. Complete them and you earn additional revenue share on top of your Per-LIVE payouts.

### How missions work

Missions reset weekly and typically include targets like:

- Stream a certain number of total hours
- Go live a minimum number of days
- Gain a target number of new followers during LIVE sessions
- Hit engagement benchmarks (comments, shares, viewer retention)

[UNVERIFIED: specific mission targets vary by creator tier and region, and TikTok has not published a complete list of possible mission types]

### The 13% bonus

When you complete all assigned missions for a given week, you earn up to 13% additional revenue share on that week's LIVE earnings. Partial completion may earn a partial bonus, though TikTok hasn't confirmed whether missions are all-or-nothing or graduated.

Combined with the Per-LIVE 40%, that's the 53% maximum revenue share.

## How to Maximize Your Earnings

### Hit the 25-minute minimum every time

This sounds obvious, but it's the most common mistake. Creators who go live for 15 or 20 minutes and end early lose their entire Per-LIVE payout for that session. Set a timer. Plan enough content or conversation to fill at least 30 minutes so you have buffer.

### Aim for 45-60 minute sessions

Reports from creators in the program suggest the payout percentage jumps meaningfully once you cross the 45-minute mark. If you can sustain engagement for a full hour, you're likely hitting close to the 40% ceiling on Per-LIVE payouts.

### Focus on follower acquisition during lives

This is the variable most creators overlook. TikTok weights new followers gained during your LIVE session heavily in its payout calculations. That means your live content should actively give new viewers a reason to follow.

Tactics that work:

- Mention what you normally post so viewers know what they'll get by following
- Pin a comment explaining your content niche
- Respond to new followers by name (the notification appears in chat)
- Offer something in your next stream that rewards return viewers

### Stream consistently for Weekly Missions

The Weekly Missions track rewards frequency. Streaming once a week won't complete most mission sets. Aim for 3-5 LIVE sessions per week if you're serious about hitting the 13% bonus.

Build a public schedule and stick to it. Tell your audience when you go live. Consistency brings returning viewers, which drives engagement metrics that feed both payout tracks.

### Keep chat active

Viewer engagement (comments, shares, reactions) factors into your Per-LIVE payout rate. Dead chat means lower payouts even if you stream for an hour.

Ways to keep chat moving:

- Ask direct questions ("What should I try next?")
- Run polls or challenges
- Respond to comments out loud so viewers feel heard
- Bring on guests or do duet lives

## Scaled LIVE Rewards vs Regular LIVE Gifts

If you already earn from LIVE gifts, you might wonder how Scaled Rewards changes things.

| Factor | Regular LIVE Gifts | Scaled LIVE Rewards |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| How you earn | Viewers send virtual gifts | Performance-based revenue share |
| Revenue share | ~50% after TikTok's cut on gifts | Up to 53% (40% Per-LIVE + 13% Missions) |
| Minimum session length | None | 25 minutes |
| Depends on | Viewer generosity | Consistency, duration, follower growth |
| Predictability | Low (varies by session) | Higher (structured targets) |
| Weekly bonuses | None | Weekly Mission completion bonuses |

The two systems stack. You still receive gifts from viewers during a Scaled LIVE Rewards session. The revenue share from Scaled Rewards is calculated separately and adds to whatever gifts you receive.

This means a strong live streamer earning gifts AND hitting Scaled Rewards targets makes more per session than someone relying on either system alone. For a deeper comparison of LIVE income vs video-based earnings, see the [LIVE gifts vs Creator Rewards breakdown](/guides/live-gifts-vs-rewards).

## Making It Work

Scaled LIVE Rewards favors creators who treat live streaming as a regular practice, not an occasional experiment. The 25-minute minimum, the weekly mission structure, and the emphasis on follower growth all point toward the same conclusion: TikTok wants committed live streamers and is willing to pay more for them.

If you already go live regularly, enabling Scaled Rewards is straightforward. Stream a bit longer, focus on converting viewers to followers, and complete your weekly missions.

If you're new to LIVE, start with 30-minute sessions three times a week. Get comfortable with chat interaction before pushing for longer streams. The revenue share scales with your skill, not just your time.
