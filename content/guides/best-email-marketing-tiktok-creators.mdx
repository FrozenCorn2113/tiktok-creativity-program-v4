## Why TikTok Creators Need an Email List

TikTok's algorithm decides who sees your content. You can post a video that gets 2 million views followed by one that gets 400. That unpredictability makes it nearly impossible to launch a product, promote a service, or build consistent income from content alone.

Email flips this. When you send an email, it lands in the inbox. Open rates for creator newsletters typically run 30 to 50%, which means a list of 1,000 subscribers puts your message in front of 300 to 500 people every time you hit send. Try getting that kind of reach consistency on any social platform.

The real payoff shows up when you sell something. A well-timed email sequence to a warm list converts at 2 to 5%. The same offer posted as a TikTok link-in-bio? Sub-1%. If you're in the [Creator Rewards Program](/guides/creativity-program-complete-guide-2026) and selling digital products on the side, email is the bridge between "making content" and "running a business."

## Where Kit Wins for Creators

### You only pay for active subscribers

Mailchimp charges you for every contact in your account, including people who unsubscribed. That means your bill goes up even as your usable list stays the same. Kit only counts active subscribers toward your plan. You pay for the people who actually want to hear from you.

### Built-in commerce

Kit lets you sell digital products directly through its platform. No separate Gumroad account. No third-party checkout page. You can create a landing page, set up a product, take payment, and deliver the file, all inside Kit. For creators selling courses, templates, ebooks, or presets, this simplifies the entire funnel.

### Creator Profile (link-in-bio)

Kit includes a Creator Profile page that works as a link-in-bio destination. It pulls together your email signup, your products, your social links, and your content. Instead of paying for a separate link-in-bio tool, Kit handles it within your existing account.

### Visual automation builder

This is where Kit saves you the most time long-term. You can build email sequences visually: "When someone downloads my free guide, wait 2 days, send the welcome sequence, tag them as interested in [topic], then add them to the product launch sequence." Mailchimp has automations too, but Kit's builder was designed for exactly this use case.

### Tagging over lists

Kit uses a tag-based system. One subscriber can have multiple tags (downloaded your free guide, purchased your course, interested in coaching). Mailchimp uses lists, and a subscriber on two different lists counts as two contacts on your bill. For creators with multiple products or content categories, Kit's approach keeps things clean and costs down.

## Where Mailchimp Still Works

Mailchimp is a solid tool for a different kind of user. If you're running a traditional e-commerce store and need deep Shopify integration, transactional emails for order confirmations, and design-heavy branded templates, Mailchimp handles that well.

It also has a more mature advertising integration if you're running Facebook or Google ads alongside email. The reporting dashboard gives you more granular data on campaign performance across channels.

But most TikTok creators don't need any of that. You need a landing page, an email sequence, a way to sell a digital product, and a system that doesn't charge you for dead contacts. That points to Kit.

## How to Set Up Kit as a TikTok Creator

Getting started takes about 30 minutes.

**Step 1: Create your free account.** Kit's free tier covers everything you need to start: landing pages, email broadcasts, basic automations, and up to 10,000 subscribers.

**Step 2: Build a landing page.** Create a simple opt-in page for your lead magnet. A free checklist, template, mini-guide, or resource list. Whatever matches your niche. This becomes your link-in-bio destination.

**Step 3: Set up your welcome sequence.** Write 3 to 5 emails that introduce yourself, deliver value, and mention your paid offers. Kit's visual automation builder makes this straightforward. Set the timing (1 to 2 days between emails works well) and let it run.

**Step 4: Connect to your TikTok funnel.** Update your bio link to point to your Kit landing page or Creator Profile. Every video you post now feeds your email list. For creators in the [Creativity Program](/guides/creativity-program-complete-guide-2026), this means your content earns RPM and builds your list simultaneously.

**Step 5: Sell something.** Once you have subscribers, use Kit's commerce feature to sell a digital product. A PDF guide, a template pack, a mini-course. The checkout lives on Kit. No additional tools required.
