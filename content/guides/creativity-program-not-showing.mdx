If Creator Rewards isn't appearing in your TikTok settings under Creator Tools, five things commonly cause it. Most are fixable. A few require time or aren't in your control. Here's how to diagnose which situation you're in and what to do about it.

<CalloutBox type="info" title="Where to look">
  Creator Rewards appears at: Profile → menu (top right) → Creator tools → Creator Rewards Program. If it's not there, one of the five reasons below explains why.
</CalloutBox>

---

## Quick Diagnostic Checklist

Before reading through each reason, run through this checklist. If you find your issue here, skip to the relevant section.

- [ ] Account type: Profile → Settings and privacy → Account → Account type. Must say "Personal Account"
- [ ] Follower count: Must be 10,000 or above
- [ ] 30-day views: Must be 100,000+ in the last 30 days (check in TikTok Studio)
- [ ] Age: Must be 18 or older
- [ ] Region: Your account's registered region must be in a supported country
- [ ] Violations: Account status must show no active warnings or strikes

If any of these fail, that's your cause. If all of them pass and Creator Rewards still doesn't appear, scroll to Reason 5 (app cache issue) or the "If None of These Apply" section.

## Reason 1: Your Region Isn't Supported

The Creator Rewards Program isn't available everywhere. TikTok has been expanding the list of supported regions over time, but as of early 2026, it's still limited to specific countries. The list changes as TikTok adds markets, so verify the current list at TikTok's Creator Portal or help center.

Supported regions at time of writing include the US, UK, France, Germany, Brazil, Japan, South Korea, and others. Check TikTok's official support documentation for the current complete list.

If your account's registered region isn't on the supported list, the program won't appear in your settings regardless of your follower count or account history.

**What you can do:** Check TikTok's current list of supported countries. If your region isn't listed, there's no workaround. The program isn't available to you yet. Monitor TikTok's official announcements for region expansions.

---

## Reason 2: Your Account Is Set to Business Type

Creator Rewards is available for Personal accounts only. If your account is set to Business type, the program won't appear, even if you're an individual creator.

Check your account type at: Profile → menu → Settings and privacy → Account → Account type.

If it reads "Business Account," that's your cause.

**What you can do:** Switch to a Personal account. Be aware that Business accounts have access to certain analytics and tools that Personal accounts don't. Review what changes before switching.

<CalloutBox type="warning">
  Switching between Business and Personal account types is allowed, but any active TikTok Ads campaigns or Business-specific settings may be affected. If you're running ads, check the implications before switching.
</CalloutBox>

For the full step-by-step conversion process, including exactly what you lose and keep, see the [convert business to personal guide](/guides/convert-business-to-personal).

## Reason 3: You Don't Meet the Follower or View Thresholds

The current requirements for Creator Rewards are:

- 10,000 followers
- 100,000 views in the last 30 days (TikTok has adjusted program requirements before; verify current thresholds at TikTok's Creator Portal)
- 18 years or older
- Personal account
- Eligible region

If you're below either threshold, the program option won't appear in your settings until you've met the requirements.

**What you can do:** Check your current follower count and your 30-day view count in Creator Studio analytics. If you're close to the threshold, the option may appear on its own once you cross it. If you're significantly below, focus on content consistency and view volume before checking again.

**How to check your 30-day views:**
TikTok Studio (studio.tiktok.com) → Overview → Video views → set date range to last 30 days. This gives you an exact number. The app's built-in analytics may show slightly different figures depending on timezone. Use TikTok Studio for the most accurate read.

## Reason 4: Your Account Has Active Content Violations

Accounts with active Community Guidelines violations or strikes won't see Creator Rewards in their settings until the violation period clears. This includes active warnings, content removals, or account restrictions.

**What you can do:** Go to Profile → menu → Settings and privacy → Account → Account status to see if there are any active strikes or violations. If there are, the Creator Rewards option may return once the violation period expires, depending on severity.

If you believe a violation was applied in error, you can appeal it through the Account status screen. Resolving or waiting out the underlying violation is the only path forward here.

The appeal process for Creator Rewards rejections (which is related but separate from content violation appeals) is covered in the [appeal rejection guide](/guides/appeal-rejection).

**How long violations last:**
Minor violations (warnings, one-time content removals) typically clear within 30 to 90 days, though duration varies by violation type and account history. Check your Account status screen for specific expiry dates. More serious strikes can stay on your record longer and may require an appeal to remove.

## Reason 5: App Cache or Account Sync Issue

Occasionally Creator Rewards simply doesn't appear due to a display glitch, especially after TikTok updates or after switching account types. This is less common than the other reasons but worth ruling out if everything else checks out.

**What to try:**

1. Clear the TikTok app cache: Profile → Settings and privacy → Free Up Space → Clear Cache
2. Log out and back into your account
3. Update the TikTok app to the current version in the App Store or Google Play
4. Check TikTok Studio on desktop (studio.tiktok.com): sometimes Creator tools appear there before updating in the mobile app
5. Try on a different device or connection if available

If the option still doesn't appear after clearing cache and updating the app, the issue is one of the four substantive reasons above, not a display glitch.

## If None of These Apply

If you meet all requirements, your account is Personal, you're in a supported region, your follower and view counts are above threshold, your account status shows no violations, and Creator Rewards still doesn't appear after clearing cache, contact TikTok support directly.

**How to contact TikTok support:**
Profile → menu → Settings and privacy → Support → Report a problem

Include:
- Your account handle
- Your current follower count
- Your 30-day view count (from TikTok Studio)
- Confirmation that your account type is Personal
- Confirmation of no active violations
- A description of what you've already tried

TikTok support response times vary, but this is the right channel for account-specific issues that the standard troubleshooting steps don't resolve.

<CalloutBox type="tip">
  If you're on the edge of the view threshold, say 80,000 to 95,000 views in the last 30 days, the option may appear and disappear depending on when TikTok checks your metrics. Keep posting consistently and check back in a few days.
</CalloutBox>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**I had Creator Rewards before and now it's gone. Why?**
If Creator Rewards disappears after you were previously enrolled, it's usually because your account fell below the ongoing eligibility thresholds (view count or follower count dropped), or your account received a new violation. Check your metrics and account status.

**Does the Creator Rewards option appear before I formally apply?**
Yes. Seeing the option in Creator Tools is the first step. You still need to tap it and complete the application process to be enrolled.

**Can I apply from outside my home region?**
The eligibility check is based on your account's registered region, not your current physical location. Traveling to an ineligible region while your account is registered in an eligible one doesn't affect your status. If you're uncertain about your account's registered region, verify with TikTok support.

**Does follower count above 10,000 improve my approval odds?**
The threshold is a minimum. Being significantly above it doesn't improve your odds in ways TikTok has made explicit. Content compliance and account health matter more than a high follower buffer.

**The option appeared once and then disappeared. Is that normal?**
Yes, if you're right at the view threshold. Creator Rewards checks your rolling 30-day views, so a dip in posting frequency or a weaker week can push you below the threshold temporarily. The option reappears once your 30-day view count is back above the minimum.

## What Happens After Creator Rewards Appears

Once you can see Creator Rewards in Creator Tools, you still need to complete the application. The application typically asks you to:
- Confirm your age
- Accept the program terms
- Verify your payment and tax information

After submitting, approval usually takes between 24 hours and a few days. If you're approved, earnings start accumulating on qualifying videos posted after your enrollment date. Videos posted before enrollment don't retroactively earn.

For a complete breakdown of eligibility requirements, what TikTok checks during the review process, and what happens after you're approved, see the [eligibility requirements guide](/guides/eligibility-requirements).
