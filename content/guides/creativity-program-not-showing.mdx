# TikTok Creativity Program Not Showing? Here's the Full Diagnostic (2026)

You can't find the Creator Rewards Program in your TikTok settings. It's either never been there, it disappeared after you had it, or it shows up greyed out and you can't actually do anything with it.

Quick note on naming before anything else: TikTok rebranded the Creativity Program to "Creator Rewards Program" in late 2024. They're the same thing. If you're searching for "Creativity Program" and not finding it because the tab now says "Creator Rewards," that's likely part of the confusion. This guide covers both naming variants and everything that might be causing the option not to appear.

The harder problem is that "not showing" means four completely different things, and each requires a different fix. Before you start troubleshooting, you need to know which situation you're actually in.

---

## The Four Situations (Pick Yours Before Reading Further)

**Situation A: Never showed up at all.** You've met what you believe to be the requirements and the option has never appeared in Creator Center. You might be missing an eligibility requirement, or your account could have a blocker you're not aware of.

**Situation B: Disappeared after you had it.** You were previously enrolled or saw the option and now it's gone. This is either a UI bug, an account status change, or your metrics dropped below an ongoing threshold.

**Situation C: Shows up but you can't apply.** The option exists but is greyed out, or you click it and get a rejection message. Something specific is blocking your application.

**Situation D: You applied, got rejected, and now the option looks different.** Post-rejection, the Creator Center shows a different state than it did before. You may be waiting out a cooling period or need to meet different requirements for re-application.

If you're in Situation B and it just happened overnight with no policy changes, start with Check 8 below before working through the full list. That's the UI bug fix and it's fast.

---

## Check 1: Are You Looking in the Right Place?

The path is: TikTok app → Profile → three-line menu (top right) → Creator Tools → Creator Rewards Program.

If you've been looking in Settings and Privacy, that's wrong. Creator Rewards lives under Creator Tools, not account settings. This resolves more cases than it should.

If the Creator Tools path doesn't show Creator Rewards, continue below.

---

## Check 2: Personal Account or Business Account?

This is a hard eligibility block. Creator Rewards is only available on Personal accounts. If your account is set to Business type, the option will not appear. Full stop.

Check at: Profile → three-line menu → Settings and Privacy → Account → Account Management. Look for your account type.

If it says Business, switch to Personal. After switching, you'll likely need to apply or re-apply for Creator Rewards rather than having it appear automatically.

One important heads-up: if you previously had Creator Rewards and switched to Business even briefly, you may have been disqualified. Getting the option back after a Business account switch sometimes requires going through the re-application process even after you've switched back to Personal. The [rewards-disabled guide](/guides/rewards-disabled) covers that re-entry path.

---

## Check 3: Your Account's Registered Country

The Creator Rewards Program is only available in specific countries. As of this writing, those include the US, UK, Germany, France, Japan, South Korea, and Brazil. This list has changed before and may change again. Verify the current list directly at TikTok's Creator Center before relying on it; this is something TikTok updates without announcement.

The important nuance: eligibility is tied to the country your account was **registered in**, not where you're physically located. A US citizen living abroad with a US-registered account is fine. A creator currently living in the US whose account was registered in Canada when they set it up may be blocked.

Check your account's registered country at: Settings and Privacy → Account → Country/Region.

If your registered country isn't on the eligible list, there's no workaround that reliably works. VPNs change your apparent login location but not the account's registered country, which is what TikTok actually checks. A creator from Costa Rica documented this specifically: CRP wasn't available because her account registration country wasn't eligible, even though she was following the steps correctly.

---

## Check 4: Age Verification

You need to be 18 or older. Age is set at account creation and tied to your date of birth on file. If your account has an incorrect birth date that puts you under 18, this is a hard block that requires updating your account information.

---

## Check 5: Follower Count

The minimum is 10,000 followers. Check your profile. If you're below this, the Creator Rewards option won't appear until you hit the threshold.

---

## Check 6: 30-Day View Count (the One People Get Wrong)

This is the requirement that trips up the most creators and causes the most "I should qualify but it's not showing" confusion.

You need 100,000 views in the **last 30 days**. Not total lifetime views. Not views from last quarter. The specific 30-day rolling window at the moment you try to access or apply.

A creator with 5 million lifetime views who had a slow month last month (say, 60,000 views) won't see the option or will get rejected for "insufficient views."

How to check: Creator Analytics → select "Last 30 days" → Total Video Views. If you're under 100,000, this is the blocker.

This metric is rolling. If you're at 75,000 views in the past 30 days, posting more content and waiting for another week or two may push you over threshold without you changing anything else.

One more thing: those views need to come from public videos. Views on private, friends-only, or restricted videos don't count toward the 30-day threshold for eligibility purposes.

---

## Check 7: Account Standing

Active Community Guidelines violations, warnings, or restrictions can block Creator Rewards access. Check: Creator Center → Creator Tools → Community Guidelines Status.

If there's an active violation, the Creator Rewards option may not appear until the violation period clears. Depending on severity, that could be days or permanently on your account's access.

---

## Check 8: The App Update Bug (Most Likely Cause if You Had It and It Disappeared)

If your Creator Rewards tab disappeared overnight and you haven't received any violation notices or policy messages, this is almost certainly a UI bug from an app update.

It happens. Multiple creators in r/TikTokMonetizing reported the same experience simultaneously: one morning, all Creator Rewards access was gone from the main app. No violations, no warnings, accounts in good standing, some with balances of $1,500–$3,000 showing as zero. The bug affected multiple accounts at the same time, pointing to a backend issue rather than individual account action.

The fix that works: download TikTok Studio (it's a separate app from the main TikTok app, available in the App Store and Google Play). Log in with your email. TikTok Studio will send a 6-digit verification code, and after login you'll find your Creator Rewards dashboard, monetization tab, and marketplace access there even if they're missing from the main app.

This was documented by community member u/Myfoodcoach on r/TikTokMonetizing and confirmed by multiple other creators in the same thread. It's not an official TikTok fix, but it's the one that reliably works.

You should also try the standard app troubleshooting: clear the TikTok app cache (Settings and Privacy → Free Up Space → Clear Cache), log out and back in, and update to the latest app version. But if those don't work, TikTok Studio is the move.

## Check 9: Prior Disqualification and Re-Application Requirements

If you were previously enrolled in Creator Rewards and then removed (either through a disqualification or a violation), you're not on the first-application path anymore. You're on a re-application path, and the requirements are different.

Re-application typically requires:
- 30 days since the disqualification date
- 100,000 new views from new public content posted after the disqualification (not just your existing view history)
- All standard eligibility requirements met: 10K followers, Personal account, eligible country, no active violations

One creator documented this sequence: accepted into CRP, disqualified before first payout, appealed and waited one month, reapplied — rejected for "insufficient public videos." They uploaded new original 1-minute+ content, accumulated 100,000 new views over several weeks, and then reapplied successfully.

If you previously applied and were rejected for "insufficient public views," the path forward is posting new eligible content and letting the 30-day view count rebuild before applying again.

---

## Why It Shows Up But You Can't Apply

Even when the Creator Rewards option appears, there are specific blockers that prevent application:

- Active violations pending review
- Insufficient public videos over 1 minute in length
- A previous application denial within a cooling period
- An account-level content quality flag that hasn't been resolved

If you can see the option but get rejected when you try to apply, the rejection message should tell you which of these is the cause. "Insufficient public views" and "insufficient public videos" are the most common, and both have the same fix: post more qualifying content and wait.

---

## If You've Checked Everything and It's Still Not There

If you've worked through all nine checks and genuinely meet every requirement — Personal account, eligible country, 10K+ followers, 100K+ views in the last 30 days from public videos, 18+, no violations, no prior disqualification — and the option still isn't appearing, contact TikTok support:

Profile → three-line menu → Settings and Privacy → Support → Report a problem.

Be specific. Include what you've already checked and what your current metrics are. Account-specific issues that don't fit a standard category do exist and they require individual support review to resolve.
