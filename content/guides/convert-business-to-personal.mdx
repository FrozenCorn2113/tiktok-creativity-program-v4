Creator Rewards isn't available on Business accounts. If you're set up as a Business on TikTok, you can't access the program until you switch. The conversion process takes about two minutes, but there are things you'll lose access to that are worth knowing about before you flip the switch.

This guide walks through the exact steps and tells you what changes on both sides.

---

## Why Business Accounts Can't Access Creator Rewards

TikTok designed Creator Rewards for individual creators, not businesses. The distinction matters to TikTok for a few reasons: Business accounts have access to TikTok Ads Manager, commercial sounds, and certain analytics tools that Personal accounts don't. Creator Rewards, in turn, is available only to Personal accounts.

If your account was set to Business, either because you set it up that way or because a third-party tool or TikTok Ads integration switched it, Creator Rewards won't appear in your settings at all. Switching back to Personal is the only way to access it.

---

## What You Lose When You Switch to Personal

Before converting, know exactly what changes.

| Feature | Business Account | Personal Account |
|---|---|---|
| Creator Rewards | Not available | Available (if eligible) |
| TikTok Ads Manager | Full access | No access |
| Commercial sound library | Full access | Limited to standard library |
| Business analytics | Extended data suite | Standard creator analytics |
| Website link in bio | Available | Available |
| Booking button in bio | Available | Not available |
| Auto-message / quick replies | Available | Not available |
| Creator Marketplace | Limited | Full access |
| TikTok Shopping | Available | Available |

The main practical losses are TikTok Ads access and the extended Business analytics suite. If you're actively running paid TikTok campaigns, pause or download your campaign data before switching. If you were using Business analytics, export any reports you need.

The commercial sound library is worth noting for content creators. Business accounts can't use most trending audio due to licensing restrictions. Personal accounts can. So if your content relies on trending sounds, switching to Personal actually expands what you can use.

## How to Check Your Current Account Type

Profile → menu (top right) → Settings and privacy → Account → Account type

If it reads "Business Account," you're on Business. If it reads "Personal Account," you're already set and Creator Rewards should be available (assuming you meet the other eligibility requirements).

## How to Convert: Step by Step

**On mobile (iOS or Android):**

1. Open TikTok
2. Go to your profile (bottom right)
3. Tap the menu icon (three horizontal lines, top right)
4. Tap "Settings and privacy"
5. Tap "Account"
6. Tap "Switch to Personal Account"
7. TikTok will show a prompt listing what you'll lose access to. Read it, then confirm

The switch is immediate. No waiting period, no review process.

**On desktop:**

Account type switching is only available through the mobile app, not through TikTok Studio.

---

## What Happens to Your Existing Content

Your existing videos, followers, engagement history, and account performance data stay intact. Switching account type doesn't reset your metrics or remove your content.

**What changes immediately:**
- Your profile will no longer show business-specific features (booking button, business category label)
- TikTok Ads Manager access is revoked
- The commercial sound library is no longer available for new videos

**What doesn't change:**
- Your follower count
- Your existing video performance data in TikTok Studio
- Your video history
- Your For You Page distribution

## How Long Before Creator Rewards Appears

After switching to Personal, Creator Rewards should appear in Creator Tools within 24 to 48 hours. TikTok hasn't published an official update timeline after account type changes, so exact timing may vary. If it doesn't appear after 48 hours, try clearing the app cache (Profile → Settings and privacy → Free Up Space → Clear Cache) and logging out and back in.

If Creator Rewards still doesn't appear after 72 hours, the issue may not be account type. Work through the [creativity program not showing guide](/guides/creativity-program-not-showing) to diagnose other possible causes.

## What If You Need Both Business Features and Creator Rewards?

This is the core tradeoff: you can't have both on the same account. Business and Personal are mutually exclusive for Creator Rewards eligibility.

Options if you need both:

**Option 1: Maintain two accounts**
Some creators operate a Personal account for content and Creator Rewards, and a separate Business account for TikTok Ads campaigns or business promotion. Both accounts must meet TikTok's account policies, and there's no rule against having both as long as they serve distinct purposes. Check TikTok's current terms of service on multiple accounts before doing this, as platform policies on this can change.

**Option 2: Prioritize Creator Rewards**
If Creator Rewards earnings are more valuable to you than TikTok Ads access, switch to Personal and stop running paid campaigns. Most individual creators don't need TikTok Ads to grow organically.

**Option 3: Use the Creator Marketplace instead**
If your Business account was used primarily for brand deal and partnership sourcing through TikTok's commercial tools, Personal accounts can access the Creator Marketplace for the same purpose. The Marketplace may actually give you more visibility to brand partners than a Business account designation.

## What About Analytics? Will I Lose My Historical Data?

Standard analytics (views, followers, engagement) carry over to the Personal account. The data doesn't disappear.

What you lose is access to the Business-specific analytics suite, which includes some additional breakdowns available only to Business accounts. If there are specific Business analytics reports you rely on, export them before switching.

After switching, your analytics will be available in TikTok Studio in the standard Creator Analytics format, which covers:
- Overview (views, followers, profile visits)
- Content analytics (per-video performance)
- Follower analytics (demographics, geography, when they're active)
- LIVE analytics (if applicable)

This is sufficient for most content strategy decisions.

## Timing Considerations

**When to switch:**
Switch before your next application to Creator Rewards, not after you've already been rejected. If you've already been rejected for account type reasons, switching is the necessary first step before reapplying.

**Don't switch mid-campaign:**
If you have active TikTok Ads campaigns running, pause them first. Switching to Personal revokes Ads Manager access, and active campaigns in an unmanaged state can continue spending without your ability to monitor or adjust them.

**Apply for Creator Rewards soon after switching:**
Don't switch and then wait six months to apply. Switch when your metrics are strong (10,000+ followers, 100,000+ views in the last 30 days) and apply within a week or two of the conversion. This way your application metrics are high and the account type issue is already resolved.

For the full eligibility checklist and everything TikTok reviews when you apply, see the [eligibility requirements guide](/guides/eligibility-requirements).
