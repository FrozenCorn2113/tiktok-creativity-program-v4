Your video has thousands of views and you're earning almost nothing. Or your view count looks normal but your Creator Rewards dashboard shows qualified views far below your total views. Both situations have specific causes, and most are fixable with content adjustments.

This guide explains how qualified views work, why they drop off, and what to do about it.

---

## What Is a Qualified View?

A "qualified view" is not the same as a view. A view counts the moment someone sees your video. A qualified view counts only when that person watches long enough to meet TikTok's minimum watch-time threshold.

TikTok hasn't published an exact percentage, but community testing and creator reports consistently suggest the threshold is somewhere in the range of 5 to 20 seconds of actual watch time [UNVERIFIED: TikTok has not officially published its qualified view threshold; this is based on community observation]. Views below this threshold count toward your public view total but contribute nothing to your Creator Rewards earnings.

This is the core of why total views and qualified views diverge.

---

## The Diagnostic Checklist

Before changing your content strategy, figure out which problem you actually have.

**Step 1: Check your Creator Rewards dashboard**
Go to TikTok Studio → Monetization → Creator Rewards Program. Look at the ratio of your total views to qualified views over the last 30 days.

- If qualified views are 60% or more of total views: your content is holding watch time well, the issue may be geographic or content type
- If qualified views are under 40% of total views: watch time is the primary problem

**Step 2: Check your average watch time**
In TikTok Studio, look at average watch time per video. Compare this across your recent posts. If your average watch time is under 5 seconds on most videos, you're losing viewers before the qualified view threshold.

**Step 3: Check your audience geography**
Creator Rewards only pays for views from supported countries. Go to TikTok Studio → Followers → Geography. If a large percentage of your audience is in regions outside the supported countries (India, Southeast Asia, much of Africa and Latin America), those views won't qualify [UNVERIFIED: TikTok's list of monetization-eligible countries changes; check the official Creator Portal for the current list].

**Step 4: Check your video length**
Videos under 60 seconds don't qualify for Creator Rewards at all. Check your recent posts. If you've been posting under 60 seconds, none of those views count regardless of watch time.

## Reason 1: Watch Time Below Threshold

This is the most common cause of low qualified view rates. Viewers tap away in the first few seconds, before reaching the watch time minimum.

**Why viewers leave early:**
- A slow hook. The first 2 to 3 seconds either hold attention or lose it. Most videos that fail do so in this window
- Front-loading context instead of value. "Today I'm going to talk about..." is a hook that loses people. Starting with the thing itself keeps them
- Poor video or audio quality. A single audio issue (echo, background noise, low volume) causes rapid tap-outs
- Unclear premise. If viewers can't tell what the video is about in the first 3 seconds, they leave

**What to fix:**
- Start with the payoff, not the setup. The answer, the result, the surprising fact, the technique, not the explanation of what you're about to explain
- Keep the first cut tight. Don't pad the opening with text animations, logos, or long intro sequences
- Test hooks by posting two similar videos with different openings and comparing watch time in TikTok Studio

For a full treatment of watch time strategy, the [maximize qualified views guide](/guides/maximize-qualified-views) covers the mechanics and specific content formats that hold attention well.

## Reason 2: Videos Under 60 Seconds

Creator Rewards only applies to videos 60 seconds or longer. A video that's 58 seconds earns nothing regardless of views or watch time.

Check your video lengths in TikTok Studio. Sort by lowest earnings to find which videos aren't qualifying.

**The fix:** For content that naturally runs short, consider whether the format fits Creator Rewards at all. Some content categories (quick tips, memes, clips) aren't compatible with the 60-second minimum. If Creator Rewards is your goal, plan content that justifies the length: tutorials, process videos, explanations, or story-based content that holds attention for at least 60 seconds without padding.

## Reason 3: Geographic Restrictions

Your view count may be high, but if a large portion of your audience is outside Creator Rewards eligible countries, those views don't qualify. You earn nothing from a viewer in a non-monetized country regardless of how long they watch.

**Common geographic patterns that reduce qualified views:**
- Viral videos that spread to global audiences often pick up large non-monetized view percentages
- Content using non-English audio or targeting non-US markets may have high international distribution
- Hashtags and sounds that are trending internationally distribute your video globally

**Check your current audience geography:**
TikTok Studio → Followers tab → Geography section. Look at your top countries by percentage.

**What you can and can't control:**
You can't fully control where your videos are distributed, but you can signal to TikTok who your content is for:
- Caption in English if you're targeting US/UK audiences
- Reference local topics (US news, trends, seasonal content) that don't translate globally
- Use trending sounds that are primarily popular in your target region

## Reason 4: Bot or Artificial Traffic Filtering

TikTok filters views it identifies as low-quality or inauthentic before calculating qualified views. If a video picks up unusual traffic patterns (very fast view spikes, extremely low engagement rates, views with no followers behind them), TikTok may strip a portion of those views from qualified view calculations.

This isn't something you can diagnose directly. But you can see it: a video with 50,000 total views and 1,000 qualified views, combined with unusually low like and comment rates, may have received traffic that TikTok filtered.

**What to do:**
- Don't purchase views. Bought views are the most common cause of artificial traffic penalties
- If you're seeing strange patterns, check TikTok Studio for engagement rates across your videos. Healthy videos typically see likes at 2 to 8% of total views; videos with much lower rates may have traffic quality issues

---

## Reason 5: Content Category Restrictions

Some content types earn lower qualified view rates because TikTok restricts their distribution in certain contexts. This includes:

- Content with music copyright issues (the video may play but TikTok may limit monetized distribution)
- Content flagged for review by the moderation system (visible in Creator Studio if a video is "under review")
- Duplicated or repurposed content

**Check for copyright flags:**
In TikTok Studio, look for any videos marked with copyright notices or sound restrictions. These can reduce qualified distribution even if the video is publicly visible.

**Check for review status:**
Videos "under review" in TikTok Studio won't accumulate Creator Rewards qualified views until the review clears.

---

## Reason 6: Creator Rewards Not Properly Enabled

Sometimes the problem is simpler: Creator Rewards isn't actually active on your account, or something changed since you enrolled.

Confirm your status:
1. Go to TikTok Studio → Monetization → Creator Rewards Program
2. Check that your status shows "Active" or "Enabled"
3. If it shows "Suspended," "Disabled," or "Under Review," that's a separate issue

If your rewards are disabled, see the [rewards disabled guide](/guides/rewards-disabled) for the reinstatement process.

## The Fix Checklist: What to Change Starting Now

| Problem | Fix |
|---|---|
| Low watch time | Rewrite your hooks. Start with value, not setup |
| Videos under 60 seconds | Check length before posting. Pad to 60s with genuine content, or accept these won't earn |
| Non-monetized audience | Caption in English, reference local topics, use region-specific trending audio |
| Copyright-flagged audio | Replace with TikTok's commercial sound library |
| Creator Rewards not enabled | Recheck enrollment in TikTok Studio |
| Bot traffic suspected | Don't buy views. Focus on organic content |

## When to Contact TikTok Support

Contact support if:
- Your qualified view count is showing as zero despite posting qualifying videos (60+ seconds, no violations)
- Your Creator Rewards status shows active but your dashboard has shown $0 for more than 14 days
- Your account shows a technical error in the Creator Rewards section of TikTok Studio

How to contact:
1. TikTok Studio → Support (bottom left)
2. Or: TikTok app → Profile → menu → Settings and privacy → Support → Report a problem

When you submit a support ticket, include:
- Which specific videos are not counting qualified views
- Your current video lengths
- Screenshots of your Creator Rewards dashboard
- Confirmation that your account is Personal type and in good standing

TikTok support response times for Creator Rewards issues vary from 1 to 7 days [UNVERIFIED]. If you don't hear back within a week, follow up through the same channel.
