# TikTok Monetization Outside the US: Complete International Guide (2026)

TikTok's monetization options are not the same everywhere. Where you live determines which programs you can access, what your RPM will look like, and which strategies will actually work for your account.

This guide is the starting point for any creator outside the US trying to understand what's available, what's off the table, and how to build income regardless of their country's eligibility status.

## Which countries have access to Creator Rewards

As of early 2026, TikTok's Creator Rewards Program is available in these countries. Confirm your current eligibility directly in the TikTok app under Creator Tools before applying, as the list has expanded over time:

- United States
- United Kingdom
- Germany
- France
- Japan
- South Korea
- Mexico
- Brazil

If your country is on this list, you can apply once you hit the eligibility thresholds. If it's not, you're in the same position as Canada, Australia, and most of the world: TikTok's direct payout system is unavailable, and you need a different approach.

TikTok has expanded this list over time, so creators in adjacent markets should check their Creator Tools section periodically for changes.

## What Creator Rewards actually pays (and why country matters)

RPM (revenue per thousand qualified views) is not a flat number. It shifts based on:

**Where your viewers are located.** A UK creator whose audience is 80% UK-based will see higher RPM than a UK creator with a globally scattered audience. Advertisers pay more to reach audiences in markets with stronger consumer spending.

**Content category.** Finance, business, and professional development content tends to attract higher ad rates than entertainment or comedy. This holds across all eligible countries.

**View quality.** Qualified views require high retention on videos over one minute. If your audience drops off at the 30-second mark on a 90-second video, many of those views won't count toward earnings.

**Seasonal patterns.** RPM typically peaks in Q4 (October to December) across all markets, driven by advertiser spending around holiday campaigns.

General RPM ranges by region, based on creator-reported data (TikTok does not publish official RPM figures by country):

| Region | Approximate RPM Range |
|---|---|
| United States | $0.40 – $1.00+ |
| United Kingdom | $0.30 – $0.80 |
| Germany | $0.25 – $0.70 |
| France | $0.20 – $0.60 |
| Brazil | $0.10 – $0.35 |
| Mexico | $0.10 – $0.30 |
| Japan / South Korea | $0.15 – $0.50 |

These figures come from creator forums and self-reported data, not official TikTok documentation. Treat them as directional, not guaranteed.

## Country-by-country: what to expect

### United Kingdom

The UK is one of the stronger performing markets for Creator Rewards outside the US. English-language content travels well internationally, which means UK creators can build audiences that span multiple high-RPM regions. UK ad rates are competitive, and the pool of brand deal opportunities is deep.

### Germany

Germany has a strong digital advertising market, and German-language content performs well domestically. Creators who build a primarily German-speaking audience tend to see consistent RPM within the eligible range. GDPR compliance affects how TikTok targets ads in Germany and the broader EU, which can influence earnings slightly compared to non-EU markets.

### Brazil

Brazil has one of TikTok's largest user bases outside the US. The platform is deeply embedded in Brazilian culture, and Portuguese-language content drives strong domestic engagement. RPM is lower than US or UK levels, but volume can compensate. Brazilian creators who build large local audiences and layer in affiliate partnerships report meaningful combined income.

### France

France is an eligible country with a strong domestic creator economy. French-language content is the primary driver of local reach. RPM is moderate by EU standards, consistent with the general regional ranges above.

### Japan and South Korea

Both countries are eligible for Creator Rewards. Each has a large, highly engaged TikTok user base, but content that performs well domestically often has lower international crossover. Creators in these markets who build bilingual content or target global niches can improve their RPM by reaching higher-value ad markets.

### Mexico

Mexico is eligible, but RPM trends lower than most other program countries due to ad market size. Mexican creators who succeed with Creator Rewards typically combine it with affiliate income and brand deals rather than relying on RPM alone.

### Canada

Canada is not currently eligible for Creator Rewards despite its proximity to the US market and similar consumer profile. Canadian creators need to rely entirely on direct monetization.

## Monetization that works in any country

If Creator Rewards is unavailable where you live, these four paths build real income without depending on TikTok's programs.

### Affiliate marketing

Affiliate marketing works regardless of your country or follower count. You recommend products or tools, include a trackable link in your bio or link-in-bio page, and earn a commission on purchases.

The highest-converting affiliate content on TikTok follows a consistent pattern: show a specific problem, demonstrate the product solving it, then give a direct reason to click. Generic reviews perform poorly.

Affiliate programs worth looking at for international creators:

- Amazon Associates (available in most countries, with localized storefronts)
- Canva affiliate program (available internationally)
- Creative tool affiliates (editing apps, stock sites, recording gear)
- Niche-specific programs in your content category

### Brand deals and sponsorships

Brands pay for access to your audience, not for your country's eligibility status. If you have consistent engagement and a clear niche, you can approach brands in your region or pitch internationally.

Build a basic media kit first: average views per video, engagement rate, audience demographics, and two or three examples of your best content. Most creators wait too long to do this. You don't need 100K followers to start.

Rates vary widely by market and niche. For creators averaging 10K–50K views per video, $200–$800 per dedicated post is a reasonable starting range, adjusted based on your niche and the brand's budget.

### Digital products

If you teach, coach, or have a specific expertise, a digital product can generate income from a relatively small audience. Templates, guides, presets, mini-courses, and swipe files all work well when they solve a specific, concrete problem.

The formula: your most common viewer question becomes your first product. If people ask the same thing in your comments repeatedly, that's your answer.

### Services and UGC

You can monetize your video skills directly by offering user-generated content (UGC) production for brands. UGC creators don't need a large following, just a clean portfolio and the ability to produce on-brief content.

Services like video editing, social media management, and content strategy are also legitimate income streams for creators who want direct payments rather than platform-dependent payouts.

## The audience location problem

One nuance that affects international creators: your audience doesn't have to match your location.

A Brazilian creator who produces content in English and builds a following that's 40% US-based will see higher RPM than a Brazilian creator whose audience is 90% local. This isn't a reason to abandon your native language, but it's worth understanding how the system weights viewers.

If you're eligible for Creator Rewards and your RPM seems lower than expected, check your audience demographics in TikTok Analytics. A scattered international audience with heavy representation from lower-RPM markets will drag your average down.

## How to check if your country has been added

TikTok occasionally expands the Creator Rewards Program to new countries. There's no reliable external announcement system for this. The only reliable way to check is directly inside the TikTok app:

1. Go to your Profile.
2. Tap the three-line menu in the top right.
3. Select Creator Tools.
4. Look for "Creator Rewards Program" in the list.

If it appears with an "Apply" or "Learn More" option, your country has access. If it doesn't appear, your country is still excluded.

## Stacking income: what the successful creators actually do

Creators who build sustainable income on TikTok outside the US don't rely on a single source. The common pattern among those doing well:

One or two affiliate programs in a specific niche, combined with occasional brand deals, and a simple product or service offer. Creator Rewards (where available) adds to that stack but doesn't anchor it.

Building direct income first, before depending on RPM, makes your business more stable regardless of what TikTok changes.
