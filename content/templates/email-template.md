# Template: Email Sequence (ConvertKit)

**Use for:** Welcome sequences, onboarding sequences, nurture sequences for new subscribers.
**Word count target:** 150–250 words per email
**Future agent note:** Email copy is slightly warmer than site content — you're writing to someone who already opted in, not a cold traffic visitor. Still direct, still no hype, but a bit more personal. Think of it as a follow-up note from the same knowledgeable peer, not a broadcast announcement.

---

## Tone Rules

**Same voice, slightly warmer register.** The site voice is a knowledgeable peer explaining something over coffee. Email voice is that same person following up the day after. It can use "I" occasionally. It can acknowledge that this stuff is confusing. It cannot be cheerleader-y, marketer-y, or use ANY of the forbidden phrases.

**Do:**
- Write to one person, not a crowd
- Use the subscriber's first name tag where natural (not forced)
- Lead with the most useful thing, then give context
- Give away real value in every email — not teasers
- Keep paragraphs short: 2–4 sentences each

**Do not:**
- Open with "I'm so excited to have you here!" or any variant
- Use subject lines that rely on mystery/clickbait ("You won't believe what I found...")
- Write a long email about how you're going to send them great emails (meta is boring)
- Add affiliate CTAs before Email 4 in any welcome sequence
- Fabricate stats or outcomes

---

## Structure Per Email

### Subject Line
- Format: Question OR specific number/fact
- Good: "What actually counts as a qualified view?"
- Good: "Your RPM explained in 3 minutes"
- Bad: "You won't believe this TikTok secret"
- Bad: "Important update inside" (vague)
- Max 50 characters preferred (mobile preview)

### Preview Text
- 1–2 sentences shown in inbox preview
- Extends the subject line, adds context
- Max 90 characters
- Example: "The number that determines your Creator Rewards payout — and why yours might be low."

### Body (3–5 short paragraphs)
- Paragraph 1: The hook — one or two sentences stating what this email is about
- Paragraph 2–4: The content — the useful thing you promised
- Final paragraph: Transition to CTA

### Single CTA
- One link per email (two maximum in Email 4 with affiliate disclosure)
- CTA copy: descriptive, not "click here"
- Good: "See how the RPM calculator works"
- Bad: "Click here for more"

### P.S. Line (optional but encouraged)
- One sentence, adds a human touch or secondary value
- Good: "P.S. The most common mistake I see creators make with this is trying to optimize RPM before they even have 10 qualified videos. Don't skip the basics."
- Don't use it as a second CTA

---

## Sequence Structure

For a 5-email welcome sequence:

| Email | Trigger | Purpose | CTA Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | Immediate (Day 0) | Deliver promised lead magnet, set expectations | Link to resource/guide |
| Email 2 | Day 2 | Quick win — teach one valuable concept | Link to calculator or guide |
| Email 3 | Day 5 | Address common frustration | Link to troubleshooting guide |
| Email 4 | Day 8 | First affiliate recommendation — transparent, relevant | Affiliate link (labeled) |
| Email 5 | Day 12 | Recap + next steps + engagement signal | Link to guides hub |

## Affiliate Disclosure Rules

- Required in every email that contains an affiliate link
- Placement: email footer (after sign-off, before unsubscribe)
- Do not hide it in tiny text
- Copy: "This email contains an affiliate link. If you purchase through it, I earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I only recommend tools I'd stand behind regardless."

```

Preview text: TikTok's payout is based on this number — not your total view count.

Hi [first_name],

Your total TikTok view count and your Creator Rewards earnings aren't directly connected.
What matters is qualified views — and they're stricter than you'd expect.

[Paragraph 1: Hook. States the counterintuitive thing immediately. No "I hope this email

A qualified view requires: the video to be at least 1 minute long, the viewer to watch a

and the view to come from an eligible region. Saves and replays may count — views from
bots or low-quality traffic don't.

[Paragraph 2: The actual content. Specific. Unverified detail is flagged.]

So if your view count is high but your qualified view count is low, the first thing to
check is your video length. Anything under 60 seconds doesn't qualify at all.

[Paragraph 3: Practical takeaway.]

The RPM calculator on the site uses qualified views — not total views — so the number it
shows you is closer to what TikTok actually pays:

→ Try the RPM calculator

[CTA: descriptive text, not "click here." Arrow format draws the eye.]

— [Name]

P.S. The most reliable way to increase qualified views is consistent video length over 60
seconds. Sounds obvious, but a surprising number of creators have one or two videos under
the threshold pulling their numbers down.

[P.S.: One sentence, genuine value, no second CTA.]
```

## Annual Refresh Protocol

1. Review all subject lines for accuracy each year — if a stat or fact is date-sensitive, update it
2. Verify all linked URLs are still live and correct
3. Verify affiliate links are still active and programs still running
4. Do not change the sequence timing without testing — the Day 0/2/5/8/12 cadence is deliberately spaced
