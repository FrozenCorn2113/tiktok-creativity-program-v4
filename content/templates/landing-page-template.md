# Template: Landing Page Copy (Email Capture)

**Use for:** Email capture conversion pages — "Start Here," lead magnets, checklist opt-ins.
**Word count target:** 150–300 words total (short and conversion-focused)
**Future agent note:** This is not a content page. It does not need comprehensive information. Its one job is to convert a visitor into an email subscriber. Every sentence either supports that conversion or should be cut. Navigation is removed on these pages — do not add it.

---

## Tone Rules

Same voice as the rest of the site — direct, plain, no hype — but with more emphasis on the reader's specific pain state. You're writing to someone who arrived confused or frustrated. Acknowledge that directly.

**Do:**
- Open with the pain state, not the solution
- Use "you" throughout
- Be specific about what they'll receive
- Trust indicators must be real — never fabricate numbers or testimonials

**Do not:**
- Use "amazing," "game-changing," "life-changing"
- Promise outcomes you can't guarantee ("You'll definitely get approved after reading this")
- Use fake testimonials or invented subscriber counts
- Add affiliate CTAs anywhere on the landing page
- Include navigation links (these distract from the conversion)

---

## Structure

### H1: Headline (pain-state focused)
- Address the specific frustration that brought them here
- Not a clever tagline — a direct statement of the problem or question
- Examples:
  - "Not sure if you qualify for TikTok Creator Rewards?"
  - "Confused about why your views aren't counting?"
  - "The TikTok Creator Rewards Program explained — without the noise"

### Subhead (value proposition)
- One sentence. What they get.
- Example: "Get the free eligibility checklist used by thousands of creators — plus weekly tips on qualified views, RPM, and payouts."
- Note: Only use social proof numbers if they are real. If the list is new, omit the number.

### Benefit Bullets (3–5 items with checkmarks)
```
✓ [Specific benefit 1 — what they'll know or be able to do]
✓ [Specific benefit 2]
✓ [Specific benefit 3]
✓ [Optional benefit 4]
✓ [Optional benefit 5]
```

Benefits should be concrete, not vague. "Know exactly which eligibility boxes you're missing" is better than "Get clarity on your eligibility."

### Email Form Context (1–2 sentences above/below the form)
- One sentence on what they're signing up for
- Example: "Enter your email and get the checklist instantly. No spam — just weekly creator updates."

### Trust Indicators (optional — only real numbers)
Use only if real data exists:
- Subscriber count (only if the number is real and current)
- "Updated for [year]" (always real)
- "Free. No credit card." (always real)

### Fine Print (1 sentence)
```
No spam. Unsubscribe any time.
```

## What NOT to Include

- Navigation menu (removed by Devan at the layout level — write copy only)
- Affiliate links
- Long explanations of the program
- Multiple CTAs competing with the email signup

## Annual Refresh Protocol

1. Update "Updated for [year]" in trust indicators each January
2. If subscriber count is now real and verifiable, add it
3. Verify benefit bullets still match what the checklist/lead magnet actually delivers
4. Do not change the headline unless conversion data indicates it's underperforming
