|---|---|---|---|---|

### RPM Context for This Niche

- Creator economy / social media tips content typically earns RPM in the $8–$20 range on mid-tier networks, $15–$35 on Mediavine, $25–$45 on Raptive. [UNVERIFIED — these are estimates based on ranges reported by publishers in similar content categories; actual RPM varies by traffic source, seasonality, and ad market conditions]
- Q4 (Oct–Dec) RPMs typically run 30–50% higher than Q1/Q2.
- TikTok creator content skews younger audience, which typically indexes lower CPM than personal finance or health content.

### Recommendation by Stage

**Now (early traffic, likely under 10K sessions/month):**
- Skip AdSense unless traffic is negligible. It trains a low-RPM floor on your site.
- Consider holding off on display ads entirely until Ezoic threshold is met.
- Focus monetization energy on affiliates, which don't require traffic minimums.

**Stage 2 (10K–50K sessions/month):**
- Apply to Ezoic. Low barrier, meaningful RPM step up from AdSense.
- Alternatively: Monumetric for a slightly more curated experience.

**Stage 3 (50K+ sessions/month):**
- Apply to Mediavine. The RPM jump from Ezoic → Mediavine is typically 2–4x.
- This is the real monetization inflection point for a content site like this.
- At 50K sessions and $20 RPM average: ~$1,000/month from display ads alone.

**Long-term (100K+ pageviews/month):**
- Raptive becomes viable and worth applying. The 25% higher RPM vs Mediavine compounds significantly at scale.
- At 100K pageviews and $30 RPM: ~$3,000/month from display ads.

**Bottom line:** Mediavine at 50K sessions is the milestone to build toward. Calculator and tool pages (which generate high engagement and return visits) are good for Mediavine's session quality requirements.

## 6. High-Revenue Content Patterns

### What Types of Pages Drive the Most Affiliate Revenue

Based on patterns across monetized content sites in adjacent niches (software reviews, creator tool guides):

**1. Product Roundup Pages ("Best X for TikTok Creators")**
- Format: "Best [category] for TikTok in 2025" with 5–10 products, comparison table, section per product
- Revenue driver: Multiple affiliate links per page; captures commercial-intent searchers mid-decision
- Conversion mechanism: Comparison tables perform significantly better than prose-only lists; readers scan to the one product that matches their need and click through
- Examples to build: "Best video editing apps for TikTok," "Best ring lights for TikTok creators," "Best microphones under $100 for TikTok" (exists but may need optimization), "Best TikTok analytics tools"
- Performance insight: These pages tend to have 3–5x higher affiliate click rates than narrative guides [UNVERIFIED — industry benchmark; verify with GA4 + affiliate dashboard once live]

**2. Comparison Pages ("X vs Y")**
- Format: Two options head-to-head with a verdict
- High commercial intent signal — user is pre-decided to buy something, just choosing which
- Revenue driver: Typically one affiliate link wins; promote that one prominently with a clear "winner" verdict
- Examples relevant here: "CapCut vs Filmora for TikTok," "Later vs Buffer for TikTok scheduling," "Epidemic Sound vs Artlist for TikTok creators"
- These rank well for low-competition long-tail queries; mid-level search volume but high conversion

**3. Calculator / Tool Pages**
- Existing calculators (earnings, RPM, follower income) are a significant strategic asset — these generate:
  - High time-on-site (user interaction)
  - Return visits (bookmark behavior)
  - Natural email capture trigger ("save your calculation" → email opt-in)
  - Strong backlink acquisition (other sites reference useful tools)
- Revenue strategy for calculators: The calculator itself shouldn't have affiliate CTAs (per the site's own monetization rules). Instead, use the calculator results page to surface contextually relevant upsells: if RPM is low → "see our guide on improving RPM" → affiliate CTAs inside that guide
- Priority enhancement: Add a "What affects your RPM?" tooltip/explainer section to the RPM calculator that links to optimize-rpm.mdx and surfaces Epidemic Sound (music licensing → qualified views) and CapCut/Filmora (video quality → completion rate)

**4. Tutorial Pages with Embedded Affiliate Links**
- Format: Step-by-step how-to with "tools I use" sidebar or inline recommendation blocks
- Best practice: Affiliate CTAs should appear after the user has received value from the guide, not in the first scroll
- Rule from strategy.md already captures this correctly: no affiliate CTAs in the Start Here hero, eligibility checklist, or FAQ answers
- Enhancement: Add "Recommended tools for this step" inline blocks to application walkthrough guides (application process = highest-intent moment for equipment/tool purchases)

**5. Troubleshooting / Problem Pages**
- "TikTok qualified views not counting" and "RPM dropping" guides fit this pattern
- Not traditional affiliate content, but these pages:
  - Attract high-intent traffic (someone frustrated with a problem)
  - Convert extremely well for email capture (they want the answer)
  - Can surface relevant tool suggestions within the solution (e.g., "use an analytics tool to track qualified view patterns" → analytics tool affiliate link)
- These pages should have the strongest email capture CTAs on the site

### CTA Placement Best Practices

- **Above-fold CTA:** Only for email capture (checklist opt-in). Do not put affiliate links above the fold.
- **Mid-content:** After solving the core problem (paragraph 4–8 in a 1,500-word guide), introduce "tool I recommend for this" inline blocks
- **End-of-guide:** Strongest affiliate CTA placement — user has read the full guide, trust is established
- **Comparison tables:** Place near the top of roundup pages (readers scroll to the table immediately)
