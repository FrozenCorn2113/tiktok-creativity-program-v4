### Current TikTok Algorithm Signals (2025–2026)
TikTok's algorithm prioritizes content distribution based on:
1. **Completion rate** — % of viewers who watch to the end. The single most important distribution signal.
2. **Rewatch rate** — Viewers who replay a video signal extremely high value.
3. **Share rate** — Videos shared to other platforms or in DMs get amplified distribution.
4. **Comment velocity** — Comment volume and speed in first hour signals conversation-worthiness.
5. **Save/Bookmark rate** — Higher save rates indicate reference-worthy content (tutorials, recipes, lists).
6. **Follower follows from non-followers** — When a video converts viewers to followers, the algorithm increases distribution.

## 5. Category-Specific Research (Growth)

### Current Algorithm Signals — Deep Dive for 5K–10K Creators

**Why completion rate matters most at this stage:**
Creators at 5K have established enough content history that TikTok's algorithm has profiled their content quality. Each new video is tested with a "seed audience" — typically the creator's existing followers plus a small cohort of new users. If completion rate in this seed audience is high, distribution expands. If low, it stays narrow.

At 5K followers, a creator has a seed audience large enough to signal quality but small enough that every video still relies on expanding to new audiences. This is the "proving ground" stage.

**The saves signal (underused by most creators at this stage):**
Tutorial and "how-to" content earns significantly more saves than entertainment content. High save rates signal to TikTok that the content is reference-worthy. This feeds ongoing distribution — the algorithm resurfaces saved content to similar users.

For 5K→10K creators: adding utility to content (a step you can save for later, a list you'll want to re-read, a tip you'll want to use next week) can meaningfully change distribution.

**The series format for 5K→10K acceleration:**
Content series create return visits. When a creator establishes a recognizable format (e.g., "One finance tip on Tuesdays") — viewers follow the account to get future installments, not just the individual video. At the 5K stage, series are significantly more effective for follower conversion than individual viral videos.
