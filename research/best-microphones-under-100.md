## 1. Current Program Details

### Market Context
Audio quality is the single most common complaint viewers cite when they stop watching TikTok videos. The mic market for TikTok creators divides cleanly into three types:

1. **USB microphones** — desk setup, excellent audio quality, wired, stationary. Best for talking-head content filmed at a desk or fixed location.
2. **Lavalier (lapel) microphones** — clip to clothing, wired or wireless. Best for on-the-go, walking tours, cooking videos, travel content.
3. **Shotgun microphones** — directional, often camera/phone-mounted. Best for outdoor content, interviews, or creators who move around the frame.

**The key TikTok constraint:** Most TikTok creators shoot on phones, not cameras. This changes the recommendation entirely — a USB microphone is irrelevant to a phone-based creator unless they're using a computer as part of their workflow. The core TikTok question is: **wired vs wireless phone microphone.**

**Wireless is increasingly dominant for TikTok:** The RØDE Wireless GO series and DJI Mic series have become the de facto standard for serious TikTok creators. Both are now available at price points approaching or slightly exceeding $100. The "under $100" query is meaningful because there are strong wired options under $100 but wireless under $100 is limited.

**Current market leaders at a glance:**
- **Wireless (at/near $100 threshold):** RØDE Wireless GO III (Gen 3) and RØDE Wireless Micro — both listed on Amazon March 2026
- **USB (under $100):** Sennheiser Profile (Wirecutter top pick, $96); Mackie EM-91CU (Wirecutter budget pick, ~$50); Maono PD200W (Wirecutter best for podcasters, ~$100)
- **Budget wired lavalier:** Multiple options at $15–$40 (TRRS for phone)
