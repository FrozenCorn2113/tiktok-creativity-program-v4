### 2. Creator Rewards Program Seasonality — RPM in Q3

**Q3 is a low-to-moderate RPM period relative to Q4.**

RPM in the Creator Rewards Program fluctuates based on advertiser demand. Q4 (October-December) consistently commands the highest RPMs as holiday ad budgets peak. Q1 is the lowest period as budgets reset. Q3 sits between these extremes — higher than Q1, lower than Q4. [FACT — multiple monetization sources including Miraflow, Bluehost, and Influencer Marketing Hub confirm this general seasonal pattern.] No precise Q3-specific RPM benchmark figure was found in public sources. [UNVERIFIED — specific Q3 RPM figures are not publicly documented.]

**Current CRP baseline rates.**

The Creator Rewards Program pays approximately $0.40 to $1.50 per 1,000 qualified views depending on niche, audience geography, and engagement quality. High-RPM niches can reach $1.50 to $2.00+. [FACT — multiple sources including Miraflow, Bluehost, Influencer Marketing Hub.] These figures reflect 2025-2026 program rates. Only videos over 1 minute qualify for payouts. [FACT — TikTok support documentation.]

**December 2025 program update impact.**

TikTok updated CRP eligibility rules in late 2025: qualified views now include search views, and only views from regions where the program is available count toward monetization. Non-eligible country views no longer count. [FACT — TikTok support pages, confirmed via multiple creator-facing sources.] This is relevant for Q3 2026 creators — audience geography now directly affects earnings in ways it did not before.

**Algorithm now rewards consistency over volume.**

As of 2026, a predictable posting schedule of three to five videos per week drives five times more engagement than high-volume dump posting. [FACT — Ingeniom, Virvid, and Syncstudio reporting on 2026 algorithm behavior.] Quality and niche consistency outperform raw frequency.
