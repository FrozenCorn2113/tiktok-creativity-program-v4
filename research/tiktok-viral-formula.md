## 1. Current Program Details

### The Virality-to-CRP Connection

"Going viral" and "earning from CRP" are related but distinct goals. Understanding the difference matters:

- **Viral reach** = high total view count. International viral videos can get millions of views with negligible CRP earnings (qualified view rate may be 5–15% if viewers are globally distributed).
- **CRP earning virality** = high qualified view rate + US/UK audience composition + completion above 1 minute. A video that "goes viral" in Brazil earns almost nothing on CRP; a video that goes viral with US audiences earns substantial CRP income.

The viral formula that matters for CRP creators is specifically: **completion-driven distribution to high-CPM audiences**.

### TikTok's Official Algorithm Documentation
From TikTok Newsroom ("How TikTok Recommends Videos for You," verified primary source):

TikTok's recommendation system ranks videos based on:
1. **User interactions** — likes, shares, comments, follows, video creation
2. **Video information** — captions, sounds, hashtags
3. **Device and account settings** — language preference, country setting, device type (lower weight)

Key official statement: "A strong indicator of interest, such as whether a user finishes watching a longer video from beginning to end, would receive greater weight than a weak indicator, such as whether the video's viewer and creator are both in the same country."

Also official: "While a video is likely to receive more views if posted by an account that has more followers, by virtue of that account having built up a larger follower base, neither follower count nor whether the account has had previous high-performing videos are direct factors in the recommendation system."

This means: **virality is achievable from any account size, and completion rate is the primary lever.**

### The Distribution Cascade Model (Community-Documented)
Community observation (consistent across multiple Reddit threads, no official confirmation) describes TikTok's distribution as a cascade of test batches:

- **Batch 1:** ~300–500 accounts. If completion rate > threshold, expand to Batch 2.
- **Batch 2:** ~1,000–5,000 accounts. Same test logic.
- **Batch 3+:** Exponential expansion if engagement continues

This model explains why videos "die at 255 views" (thread /1oq9d7u/ description) — the video failed the first batch test and was not expanded. It also explains why the initial audience quality matters: a video served to the wrong initial batch (low engagement potential) may fail even with good content.

[NOTE: This cascade model is community consensus — it is not publicly confirmed by TikTok in official documentation. TikTok has confirmed "test audience → expand based on engagement" as a general principle without batch-size specifics.]

### TikTok Search as a Virality Layer
Beyond FYP distribution, TikTok Search creates a secondary virality pathway:
- Videos that rank for search queries get continuous long-tail views indefinitely (unlike FYP which has a distribution window)
- Search RPM is reportedly higher than FYP RPM [community consensus, UNVERIFIED — see optimize-rpm]
- Search virality is slower-burn but compounds over time — more relevant to CRP earnings than one-time FYP spike

## 5. Category-Specific Research (Strategy Guide)

### The Four-Variable Viral Model for CRP Creators

**Variable 1: Topic Selection (Highest Impact)**
Community data (faceless channel thread, /1rq2l97/) is clear: topic selection beats production quality.
- Topics that reliably trigger completion: curiosity + fear + desire overlap ("things manipulators say on a first date" hits curiosity AND fear simultaneously)
- High-completion topic signals: psychological insight, social proof/comparison, how-to with clear outcome, lists with curiosity gap
- Low-completion topic signals: general information without stakes, content that doesn't reward watching to the end

**Variable 2: Completion Architecture (High Impact)**
Structural choices that drive watch-through:
- Promise in the hook that requires the full video to pay off
- Chapter/list structure that creates "just one more" momentum
- Pattern interrupts every 15–20 seconds (visual change, B-roll, text overlay shift)
- Strong ending — videos that end with "so anyway" lose rewatches; videos that end with a memorable line get replayed

**Variable 3: Initial Engagement Velocity (Medium Impact)**
The test batch judges engagement in the first few hours. Tactics that improve initial engagement:
- Posting when your most engaged followers are online (see posting-schedule research)
- Using a keyword in the caption that creates comment-worthy content ("comment 'yes' if this is you")
- Responding to comments quickly after posting — early comment replies signal active engagement

**Variable 4: Distribution Type Selection (Medium Impact)**
FYP vs. Search — different strategies, different revenue profiles:

| | FYP Virality | Search Virality |
|---|---|---|
| Speed | Fast (days) | Slow (weeks–months) |
| Traffic pattern | Spike then drop | Steady compound growth |
| CRP earnings timeline | One-time large payment | Consistent monthly income |
| Audience quality | Variable (depends on who's on FYP) | Higher intent (searched for it) |
| Optimization | Hook quality, trend timing | Keyword research, niche titles |
| Best for | Follower growth, brand awareness | Long-term CRP income |

A CRP creator optimizing for income (not just growth) should build a library of Search-optimized content alongside FYP-targeted content.

### The Completion Rate Cascade (Primary Revenue Mechanism)
1. **Strong hook** → viewer doesn't scroll (watch time starts)
2. **Retention structure** → viewer watches past 1 minute (qualified view threshold met)
3. **Rewatch trigger** → viewer replays (additional qualified view + algorithm signal)
4. **Algorithm expansion** → video served to wider audience (more qualified view opportunities)
5. **US/UK audience reach** → each qualified view worth more (higher CPM geography)
6. **CRP payment** → qualified views × RPM = earnings

Each step is a multiplier. Improving hook quality improves step 1. Improving retention structure improves step 2–3. Choosing the right niche improves step 5.

### Community-Documented Virality Killers
From thread /1p5k29u/ and /1r9rapl/:
- Videos that get views but don't convert viewers to followers → no compound effect
- International viral spikes → large view count, tiny CRP earnings (the "1M views, $30" scenario)
- Over-polished videos that prioritize production over topic relevance
- Trending sounds that pull the wrong audience
- Click-through-rate hooks that don't deliver on the implicit promise (viewers leave before 1 minute, killing qualified view rate)

### The "Repeatable Format" Principle
Multiple community threads document the same pattern: creators who found a repeatable format structure earned more consistently than those chasing one-off viral hits.

Elements of a repeatable format:
- Same video structure every time (hook style, content flow, CTA)
- Same niche and audience (algorithm learns who to show your content to)
- Same visual identity (branded elements create recognition and trust)
- Same production workflow (sustainability prevents burnout and posting gaps)
