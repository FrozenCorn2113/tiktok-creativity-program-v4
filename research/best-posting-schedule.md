## 1. Current Program Details

### How Posting Schedule Relates to CRP Earnings

TikTok's CRP pays per qualified view (1 minute of watch time on a 1-minute+ video). The mechanism connecting posting time to CRP earnings is indirect but real:

1. **Initial distribution window matters.** When a video is posted, TikTok serves it to a small test audience first. If that initial group engages — watches through, shares, replays — the algorithm uses those signals to expand distribution. A video that enters this test window during low-activity hours competes for fewer viewer slots but also gets less engagement momentum. Most industry evidence points to evening posting (US time) producing better initial engagement signals.

2. **CRP qualification is tied to watch behavior, not posting time.** Posting time does not directly affect RPM. What it affects is the size and composition of your initial audience — which affects qualified view accumulation.

3. **Consistency is documented by TikTok as an algorithmic signal.** TikTok officially recommends posting 1–4 times per day on their platform guidance. The platform's recommendation engine weights "account activity" as a signal — dormant accounts lose distribution momentum.

4. **Time zone targeting matters for US/UK audience.** Since CRP qualified views weight US/UK/DE/FR audiences more heavily (higher advertiser CPMs), posting during US evening hours is specifically relevant to earnings, not just general reach.

### TikTok's Official Position on Posting Frequency
- TikTok's own Creator Academy recommends posting 1–4 times daily [source: TikTok Creator Academy general guidance, JS-rendered — could not confirm exact URL]
- TikTok Analytics (Followers tab in TikTok Studio) shows audience activity by hour — this is the primary tool for schedule optimization
- TikTok has publicly acknowledged watch time, completion, and re-watches as stronger algorithm signals than follower count (TikTok Newsroom: "How TikTok Recommends Videos" — verified, newsroom.tiktok.com)

## 5. Category-Specific Research (Strategy Guide)

### The Two Variables: Timing vs. Frequency

Most "best time to post" guides collapse these into one answer. They're distinct:

**Timing** (when to publish) — affects who sees your video in the initial distribution window.
**Frequency** (how often to publish) — affects algorithm momentum, compound growth, and CRP qualified view volume.

Community evidence strongly suggests **frequency matters more than timing for CRP earnings**. More videos = more qualified view opportunities, even if individual videos are sub-optimal timing-wise.

**Summary pattern:** Evening ET (5–10pm) is consistently recommended Tue–Thu. Saturday midday and Sunday morning appear in multiple datasets. Afternoons (12–5pm on weekdays) consistently underperform.

**CRP-specific adjustment:** For creators targeting US CPM, shift these windows to US Eastern Time regardless of your own time zone. A UK creator posting at 7pm BST is posting at 2pm ET — mid-afternoon, which underperforms. UK creators who want US audiences should post at 10pm–1am BST.

### Frequency Recommendations

- **TikTok's stated guidance:** 1–4 times per day
- **Community consensus for CRP:** 1–2x/day is the sustainable sweet spot
- **Phase-specific guidance:**
  - Pre-10K followers: Prioritize quality over frequency. 1 good video > 3 mediocre ones.
  - 10K–50K (early CRP): 1–2x/day with consistent format. Consistency > optimization.
  - 50K+ (established CRP): Can test frequency experiments. 2x/day with niche focus.

### The "Consistency vs. Optimization" Debate

Community data is clear: **consistency beats timing optimization**. The creators who grew their channels and CRP earnings fastest in Reddit threads consistently cited:
- Posting every day (even if not at "optimal" time)
- Batching and scheduling (Sunday production batch for the week)
- Not skipping days even when views were low

The TikTok algorithm gives distribution momentum to active accounts. A 30-day posting streak likely matters more than posting at exactly 7pm vs 6pm.

### Posting Time Matters More When:
- You have an established audience with known peak activity hours (use TikTok Studio)
- You're launching a high-effort video and want maximum initial exposure
- You're doing a campaign tied to a specific event or trend
