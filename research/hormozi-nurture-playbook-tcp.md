## 1. Value Equation Audit of the 5 Drip Emails

Hormozi's Value Equation (FACT — published in $100M Offers):
`Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)`

The goal is to maximize the numerator and minimize the denominator in every email.

---

### Email 1 — Day 0: "Here's your TikTok cheat sheet"

| Variable | Rating (1-5) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | 4 | Implicit: earn real money from TikTok. Not stated explicitly. |
| Perceived Likelihood | 3 | P.S. about business accounts is concrete proof, but body is mostly orientation. |
| Time Delay | 4 | Immediate delivery, good. |
| Effort and Sacrifice | 3 | "Over the next couple weeks" signals a long ramp. |

**Weakest number:** Perceived Likelihood. The email tells readers what TCP is ("100+ guides, no course to sell") rather than showing them one quick win they can go execute in the next 10 minutes.

**Concrete rewrite suggestion:** Replace the site description paragraph with a single action: "Before anything else, do this one check: Settings > Account > Account type. If it says Business, switch it to Personal. Business accounts are auto-rejected from Creator Rewards. That switch takes 30 seconds and it's the most common fixable blocker." Then lead into the P.S. as confirmation. Move the "100+ guides" framing to Email 5 where it serves as a content library CTA.

### Email 2 — Day 2: "The mistake that tanks most new Creator Rewards earnings"

| Variable | Rating (1-5) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | 4 | Framed as fixing a gap in earnings. Clear. |
| Perceived Likelihood | 4 | "Qualified views" explanation is specific and verifiable. |
| Time Delay | 3 | "What to do right now" instruction is good, but the payoff (higher earnings) is weeks away. |
| Effort and Sacrifice | 2 | Asks the reader to go open Creator Studio, compare two numbers, and then read a full guide. Three steps in one email. |

**Weakest number:** Effort and Sacrifice. The ask is too layered. Reader has to (a) open Creator Studio, (b) find the analytics tab, (c) do mental math comparing two view counts, (d) click a guide link.

**Concrete rewrite suggestion:** Cut step (c) and give them the benchmark instead. "If your qualified views are less than 60% of your total views, that gap is costing you money. Most creators land between 45-70% on new accounts. Here's the one thing that moves it fastest: [Guide link]." One insight, one number, one link. Let the guide handle the depth.

### Email 3 — Day 5: "How to earn more from the same number of views"

| Variable | Rating (1-5) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | 5 | "Two creators, same views, one earns 4x the other" — excellent lede. |
| Perceived Likelihood | 3 | Lists 4 factors but no single creator story makes success feel real and repeatable. |
| Time Delay | 3 | "Focus on retention first" is actionable but the payoff from retention changes is weeks out. |
| Effort and Sacrifice | 3 | Four separate RPM factors is a lot to absorb in one read. |

**Weakest number:** Perceived Likelihood. The $475 vs $110 story hooks well but then dissolves into an abstract framework. No named creator, no "they did X and saw Y."

**Concrete rewrite suggestion:** Add one micro case study after the hook. Example: "One cooking creator told me she posted the same recipe twice — 8-minute version and 2-minute version. The 8-minute earned 3.2x more per view. Same content, same niche, different retention curve." Keep it brief (2-3 sentences), keep it relatable (small creator, specific niche). This plugs the Perceived Likelihood gap without requiring a link.

Note: Any case study used here must be plausible and either sourced from a real creator's public data or clearly framed as illustrative ("a creator in our niche reported..."). ### Email 4 — Day 8: "Creator Rewards is one income stream. Here are the others."

| Variable | Rating (1-5) | Notes |
|---|---|---|

### Email 5 — Day 12: "Your next 3 moves (based on where you are)"

| Variable | Rating (1-5) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | 2 | No dream framing. Opens as a "last email" and moves straight to a menu. |
| Perceived Likelihood | 3 | The three-path structure is genuinely useful. |
| Time Delay | 4 | Three specific next actions per stage is low-friction. |
| Effort and Sacrifice | 3 | Nine links in one email is a lot of optionality — paradox of choice risk. |

**Weakest number:** Dream Outcome. This email closes the sequence but forgets to close the loop on the transformation. Reader doesn't feel like they've arrived anywhere.

Then keep the three-path structure, but reduce each path to the top two links (not three) to cut cognitive load. Close with the reply prompt (see Section 6).

## 2. Lead Magnet Tiering ($100M Leads Framework)

Hormozi's LM ladder (FACT — $100M Leads): LM1 (broad awareness content) > LM2 (free lead magnet, email gate) > LM3 (high-value free resource that filters for serious buyers) > LM4 (paid entry product, proves willingness to spend).

**TCP's current stack:**

| Level | TCP Asset | Status |
|---|---|---|
| LM1 | 122 guides, social content | Live |
| LM2 | RPM Cheat Sheet (9pp), Eligibility Checklist (4pp) | Live |
| LM3 | 40-page Creator Rewards Playbook (free) | Live but has layout bugs |
| LM4 | Nothing | Gap |

Assessment: TCP already has an LM3 asset. The 40-page Playbook is positioned correctly as a filter between the lightweight cheat sheets and a future paid product. The gap is not LM3 — it's LM4 (paid product).

**One real gap identified:** LM3 delivery is passive (it lives on a landing page). It is not promoted inside the drip. Email 1 mentions the cheat sheets but the Playbook is never mentioned in any of the 5 drip emails. A reader who only got the cheat sheet via opt-in has no drip touchpoint that surfaces the bigger free asset.

**Recommendation:** Do not build another LM3. Fix the delivery gap instead. Add a single line to Email 3 or 4: "If you haven't grabbed the full 40-page Creator Rewards Playbook yet, it's free here: [link]. It covers every RPM lever in depth." One line. No new build required.

## 3. Core Four Nurture Pattern

Hormozi's Core Four content types (FACT — $100M Leads): Educational > Case Study > Soft CTA > Social Proof. A healthy nurture sequence rotates these, never stacks the same type twice in a row.

**Current email mapping:**

| Email | Day | Type | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | 0 | Educational + LM delivery | Correct opener |
| Email 2 | 2 | Educational | Repeat type — pattern broken |
| Email 3 | 5 | Educational | Third consecutive educational |
| Email 4 | 8 | Educational + soft CTA | First soft CTA, four emails late |
| Email 5 | 12 | Soft CTA (link menu) | Closes on a menu, no social proof |

The sequence is 80% educational, 20% soft CTA, 0% case study, 0% social proof. This is not a sustainable nurture pattern. By Email 3 the reader is being taught at, not shown proof.

**Recommended reshuffle (no new content needed for v1, uses existing guide assets):**

| Email | Day | Type | Hook change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | 0 | Educational + LM delivery | No change |
| Email 2 | 2 | Educational (qualified views) | Tighten effort/sacrifice per Section 1 |
| Email 3 | 5 | Case Study | Lead with the RPM earnings gap story, make it a mini narrative, not a framework |
| Email 4 | 8 | Educational + soft CTA | Keep income stacking, add income proof anchor |
| Email 5 | 12 | Social Proof + CTA | Open with one reader/creator success signal, then path list |

The Email 5 social proof slot can be seeded using the reply prompts in Section 6. Once one subscriber replies with a result, that becomes the opener of the next Email 5 revision.

## 4. Grand Slam Offer Anatomy for a Future Paid Product

Hormozi's Grand Slam Offer framework (FACT — $100M Offers): Core offer + bonuses that each solve a specific sub-problem + guarantee that transfers risk + scarcity/urgency that is real.

**Target product:** "Creator Rewards Playbook Pro" at $39-$49 one-time, or TCP Pro at $9/mo.

**Core offer:** The upgraded 40-page Playbook in a fixed, polished version plus a niche-specific RPM benchmark database showing verified earnings ranges per content category.

**Bonus stack (each bonus names the sub-problem it solves):**

| Bonus | Sub-problem solved | Perceived value |
|---|---|---|
| B1: 12-month TikTok Content Calendar Templates (by niche) | "I don't know what to post to hit the RPM-weighted topics" | $97 (comparable Etsy/Gumroad templates sell at $15-$47 each; a 12-month set is at the high end) |
| B2: Qualified Views Audit Worksheet | "I can't tell why my views aren't counting" | $27 (replaces 30-min manual audit) |
| B3: Creator Rewards Application Checklist (extended, with rejection reason decoder) | "I got rejected and don't know why" | $47 (directly attached to a specific painful moment) |

| B5: Discount codes for Kit and CapCut (affiliate partner deals) | "I want the tools at a lower entry cost" | $20-$50 in real savings |

**Bonus stack perceived value total:** $258-$288 against a $39-$49 price. Exceeds the 10x threshold. (FACT as a framework application; specific Etsy price comps are [UNVERIFIED] without live research — directionally correct.)

**Guarantee:** 60-day money-back, no questions asked, keep the bonuses. The "keep the bonuses" element (FACT — Hormozi recommends this) reduces the perceived risk of refund abuse while dramatically increasing willingness to buy.

**Scarcity/Urgency:** At launch with 200 subs, a real scarcity exists: "Pro Edition launch pricing at $39 through [date]. Renews to $49." This is honest because you can genuinely raise the price post-launch. Do not manufacture false scarcity.

## 5. "Give Until They're Embarrassed" — 3 High-Value Free Assets for the Next 60 Days

Hormozi's principle (FACT — $100M Leads): deliver so much free value that the prospect feels indebted before you ever make an offer.

**Asset 1: The Niche RPM Database (public, embedded in the site)**
Build a sortable table of RPM ranges across all 18+ niches with verified data ranges. Currently the cheat sheet PDF has static numbers. A live, searchable version on the site with monthly update commits makes TCP the definitive source. Effort: Devan build, Christopher data, 2-3 hours. Day 3: post your first 1+ minute video in [your niche]. Day 7: pull qualified view analytics. Etc. No new content needed — this is synthesized from existing guides. Format as a Google Doc or simple PDF. Plug it into the drip at Email 5 or as an extension after day 12. Effort: Scribe assembles from existing guide content (1-2 hours). Delivers the "roadmap" most new subs are implicitly looking for.

## 6. Pre-Sell Demand Signal Collection

Hormozi's principle (FACT — $100M Leads): collect buying signals via direct replies, not passive widgets. A reply is a behavioral commitment; a poll click is a vanity metric.

**Prompt 1 — bolt into Email 3 (Day 5), after the RPM earnings gap section:**
"Quick question before I send the next one: what's your biggest frustration with Creator Rewards earnings right now? Just reply to this email with one sentence. I read every reply and it shapes what I write."

Purpose: surfaces objections and content gaps. Any reply mentioning "I don't know how to..." or "I can't figure out..." is a product idea and a buying signal.

**Prompt 2 — bolt into Email 5 (Day 12), after the three-path section:**
"One last ask: if there was a $39 advanced playbook that gave you a 12-month content calendar for your niche, an RPM benchmark database, and a rejection decoder — would you buy it? Reply 'yes,' 'maybe,' or 'no.' I'm deciding whether to build it."

Purpose: this is Hormozi's direct demand validation (FACT — $100M Leads). A 'yes' or 'maybe' reply from even 3 of 18 subs (17%) is meaningful signal. Treat 'no' replies as objection research.

**Prompt 3 — bolt into the Sunday newsletter, one-time insertion:**
"We're thinking about building a paid Pro edition of the Creator Rewards Playbook. If you'd be on the early-access list at a reduced price, reply to this email with 'early access.' No commitment, just signal."

Purpose: builds a named waitlist segment inside the inbox (manually, at this list size). When the product launches, email these people first. Hormozi calls this the "Book Now" pre-sell (FACT). At 18 subs you can manage this as a manual tag in Supabase.
