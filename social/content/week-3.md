## Tuesday (Day 16)

### TikTok -- Video Concept (CT6: Myth Buster)
**Topic:** Posting 3x a day won't fix bad content

**Script outline:**
- Myth on screen: "The algorithm rewards high posting frequency"
- Correction: Quality and retention matter more than volume. Posting 3x daily with 20% retention is worse than 1x daily with 60% retention.
- Proof: TikTok's distribution system evaluates each video independently. More videos just means more chances to fail if the content isn't holding attention.
- Close: "Post less, make it better. That's the algorithm hack nobody wants to hear."

**Caption:** Posting 3x a day won't fix bad content. Here's what the algorithm actually cares about.

**Hashtag Set:** B (TikTok)
**Duration:** 60+ seconds

### X -- Thread (CT4: Algorithm Insight)
**Topic:** The 60-second rule and why it changes everything about your TikTok strategy

- **Tweet 1:** Videos under 60 seconds can't earn from TikTok Creator Rewards. That one rule changes everything about how you plan content.
- **Tweet 2:** Before you knew this, short clips made sense. Quick hooks, fast cuts, 15-second punches. But none of those qualify for payouts.
- **Tweet 3:** The shift: plan your content to run 60-90 seconds minimum. Use hooks, open loops, and pattern interrupts to keep retention high across the full length.
- **Tweet 4:** This doesn't mean padding your videos. It means choosing topics with enough depth to fill 60+ seconds naturally. If your topic only needs 20 seconds, pick a bigger topic.

**Hashtag Set:** E (X) -- first tweet only
