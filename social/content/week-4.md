### Instagram -- Carousel (CT2: Quick Tip)
**Topic:** How to find your best-performing content and make more of it

**Slides:**
- Slide 1: "Your analytics are telling you what to make next. Here's how to listen."
- Slide 2: Open TikTok Analytics and go to your Content tab.
- Slide 3: Sort by qualified views, not total views. Total views include unqualified traffic that doesn't affect your earnings.
- Slide 4: Identify patterns in your top 5 videos. What topics, formats, and hooks are recurring?
- Slide 5: Double down on what works. If 3 of your top 5 are tutorial-style, make more tutorials.
- Slide 6: "Cut what doesn't perform. If a content type consistently underperforms, drop it. Save this."

### X -- Thread (CT7: Trending Sound/Format)
**Same topic, thread format:**

- **Tweet 1:** 5 things I wish I knew before joining TikTok's Creator Rewards Program.
- **Tweet 2:** 1. "Qualified views" and "views" are different. Only views where the person watches for a meaningful duration count toward your payout. A video with 1M views might only have 400K qualified.

- **Tweet 6:** 5. You have to opt in manually. If you were on the old Creator Fund, you need to switch. It's in Settings > Creator tools > Creator Rewards Program.

**Topic:** How to repurpose one TikTok video across 4 platforms

**Post copy:**
You don't need to create unique content for every platform. Here's how to turn one TikTok into 4 pieces of content.

1. TikTok: Post the original video (60+ seconds for Creator Rewards eligibility).
2. Instagram Reel: Re-upload with an Instagram-optimized caption. Different hashtags. Same video.
3. Facebook Reel: Upload with captions burned in (viewers on Facebook are more likely to watch on mute). Add a sentence or two of context above the video.

One video, four platforms, four different audiences. The content is the same, the packaging changes.

If you signed up for Meta's Creator Fast Track, the Facebook and Instagram reposts might qualify for bonus payouts during the first 90 days. Worth checking.

## Friday (Day 26)

### TikTok -- Video Concept (CT6: Myth Buster)
**Topic:** Hashtags don't work the way you think on TikTok

**Script outline:**
- Myth on screen: "Using 15+ hashtags helps the algorithm find your content"
- Correction: TikTok's algorithm classifies content primarily through video analysis, not hashtag text. 3-5 relevant hashtags for discoverability is enough.
- Proof: The algorithm watches what's in your video, listens to the audio, and tracks viewer behavior. Hashtags are supplementary signals, not primary ones.
- Close: "Stop stuffing hashtags. Write a good caption and use 3-5 relevant tags."

**Caption:** You're using too many hashtags. Here's how many you actually need.
