## System Overview

7 reusable content templates (CT1-CT7), a 28-day content calendar, rotating hashtag sets for 5 platforms, and a Reddit participation guide. All content is copy-paste ready for Wren to schedule.

**Voice:** Creator-native. First person. No brand-speak. No AI vocabulary. Community-reported data labeled correctly.

**Content ratio (weeks 1-4):** 100% value content. Zero promotional posts. Zero TCP links in social captions.

## CT1: Earnings Update

**Pillar:** Creator earnings milestones
**Best for:** Instagram, TikTok, X, Facebook

### Structure

```
EARNINGS HOOK: [Specific earnings milestone or data point that stops the scroll]

CONTEXT: [1-2 sentences explaining what drove this result]

WHAT MAKES THIS WORK: [The specific factor -- niche, geography, retention, etc.]

CTA: [Platform-appropriate call to action]

SOURCE: [Community-reported / official data / mark UNVERIFIED if needed]
```

### Platform Versions

**Instagram:**
- Single image with earnings figure as large text, or carousel breaking down the factors
- Caption: 125-150 chars, earnings hook first, keyword-rich
- Hashtags: 3-5 from rotation sets (1-2-2 rule)

**TikTok (video):**
- Open with the earnings number on screen text
- Explain context in voiceover, break down the factors
- Caption: First 100 chars = the earnings hook, keyword-optimized
- 60+ seconds, 3-5 hashtags

**X:**
- Tweet: "[Earnings data point]. [One-line context]. [What to learn from it]."
- 1-2 hashtags max

**Facebook:**
- 100-250 word expanded version with more context on the factors
- No hashtags or 1-2 max

**Reddit:**
- Share earnings data only as supporting evidence in relevant threads
- Always label as community-reported, never claim personal earnings

### Example Fill

```
EARNINGS HOOK: Creator Rewards pays $0.40-$1.00 per 1K qualified views. The old Creator Fund paid $0.02-$0.04.

CONTEXT: That's a 10-20x increase in per-view earnings. The gap between these two programs is not small, and if you're still on the old Creator Fund, switching is the single biggest pay raise available to you right now.

WHAT MAKES THIS WORK: The Creator Rewards payout model is tied to qualified views and RPM, which means your niche, audience geography, and watch time all directly affect your rate.

CTA: Check your eligibility in your TikTok settings today.

SOURCE: Community-reported data across multiple forums. RPM varies by niche and region. [UNVERIFIED: exact range based on community reports, not official TikTok data]
```

## CT2: Quick Tip

**Pillar:** Actionable creator tips
**Best for:** Instagram carousel, TikTok video, Pinterest infographic, X thread

### Structure

```
HOOK: [One-line question or bold claim about the tip topic]

STEPS:
1. [Step with specific action]
2. [Step with specific action]
3. [Step with specific action]
4. [Optional: Step with specific action]
5. [Optional: Step with specific action]

TAKEAWAY: [One sentence summarizing the outcome of following these steps]

CTA: [Platform-appropriate call to action]
```

### Platform Versions

**Instagram (carousel):**
- Slide 1: Hook question as headline
- Slides 2-5: One step per slide with supporting detail
- Final slide: Takeaway + "Save this for later" CTA
- Caption: 125-150 chars summarizing the tip, keyword-rich
- Hashtags: 3-5 from rotation sets (1-2-2 rule)

**TikTok (video):**
- First 3 seconds: Hook question on screen text
- Caption: Hook in first 100 chars, keyword-optimized
- Hashtags: 3-5 from TikTok rotation sets
- Video must be 60+ seconds (Creator Rewards eligibility)

**X (tweet or thread):**
- Single tweet: Hook + compressed steps (200-250 chars)
- Thread version: Hook tweet, then one tweet per step
- 1-2 hashtags max

**Pinterest (infographic pin):**
- Keyword-rich title: "How to [Topic] on TikTok in [Year]"
- 2:3 ratio design (1000x1500px)
- SEO description with long-tail phrases
- 2-5 hashtags in description

**Facebook (text post):**
- 100-250 word expanded version with context
- No hashtags or 1-2 max

**Reddit (comment format):**
- Standalone value, no links, no promotional language
- Written as helpful advice from a fellow creator

### Example Fill

```
HOOK: Your TikTok RPM is probably lower than it should be. Here's why.

1. Check your audience location in Analytics > Followers. US/UK/Canada viewers pay 5-10x more than other regions.
2. Post when your target geography is awake, not when you're awake. Use Analytics > Follower Activity.
3. Stop posting under 60 seconds. Videos under 1 minute don't qualify for Creator Rewards at all.
4. Drop trending sounds if they don't match your niche. Relevance beats virality for RPM.

TAKEAWAY: RPM comes from where your viewers are and how long they watch, not how many views you get.

CTA: Save this and check your analytics tonight.
```

## CT3: Tool Review

**Pillar:** Creator tools and resources (editing, analytics, scheduling)
**Best for:** Instagram, Pinterest, X, Facebook

### Structure

```
TOOL: [Tool name]
WHAT IT DOES: [One sentence, plain language]
WHO IT'S FOR: [Specific creator type or use case]
STANDOUT FEATURE: [One thing that makes it worth using]
VERDICT: [Honest 1-2 sentence assessment]
LINK: [Affiliate link placeholder or direct URL]
```

### Platform Versions

**Instagram:**
- Single image or carousel showing tool in use
- Caption: Tool name + verdict + who should use it (125-150 chars)
- Hashtags: 3-5, rotation set

**X:**
- Tweet: "[Tool name] does [thing] for [who]. Worth it if [condition]. Not worth it if [condition]."
- 1-2 hashtags

**Pinterest:**
- Title: "Best [Tool Category] for TikTok Creators [Year]"
- SEO description with long-tail keywords
- 2:3 ratio pin

**Facebook:**
- 100-250 words with honest pros/cons
- 1-2 hashtags max

**Reddit:**
- Only share if someone asks for tool recommendations
- Never lead with affiliate links
- Describe the tool honestly, mention drawbacks

### Example Fill

```
TOOL: CapCut
WHAT IT DOES: Free video editor with auto-captions, templates, and effects built for short-form video.
WHO IT'S FOR: TikTok creators who want polished edits without paying for Premiere Pro.
STANDOUT FEATURE: Auto-captions are surprisingly accurate, and you can style them to match your brand in seconds.
VERDICT: Best free editor for TikTok. The desktop version handles longer edits better than mobile. Export quality is solid.
LINK: [affiliate link or capcut.com]
```

## CT4: Algorithm Insight

**Pillar:** What's working now on the platform
**Best for:** TikTok, X, Instagram, Facebook

### Structure

```
INSIGHT: [Specific observation about what the algorithm is currently rewarding or punishing]

EVIDENCE: [What you're seeing -- data point, pattern, or observable behavior]

ACTION STEP: [What creators should do with this information right now]

CTA: [Platform-appropriate call to action]

SOURCE: [Where this came from -- mark UNVERIFIED if not confirmed]
```

### Platform Versions

**TikTok (video):**
- Insight on screen text, explain evidence in voiceover
- Caption: First 100 chars = the insight hook
- 60+ seconds, 3-5 hashtags

**X:**
- Tweet: "[Insight]. [Evidence in one line]. [Action step]."
- 1-2 hashtags

**Instagram:**
- Single image with the insight as large text, or Reel explaining it
- Caption: Context + actionable takeaway (125-150 chars)
- Hashtags: 3-5, rotation set

**Facebook:**
- 100-250 words expanding on the implications and evidence

**Reddit:**
- Share in relevant discussion threads as supporting evidence for a point

### Example Fill

```
INSIGHT: TikTok's distribution system tests your video in a pool of 200-1,000 viewers first. Your performance there determines everything.

EVIDENCE: Videos that hit 70%+ completion rate in the initial pool consistently break into broader distribution. Videos below 40% stay flat regardless of hashtags or posting time.

ACTION STEP: Optimize your first 3 seconds. That's where most drop-offs happen. If your hook doesn't land in the initial pool, the rest of the video doesn't matter.

CTA: Check your completion rates in TikTok Analytics. What's your average?

SOURCE: Observed distribution patterns across creator accounts. [UNVERIFIED: specific thresholds are community-reported, not officially confirmed by TikTok]
```

## CT5: Success Story

**Pillar:** Creator spotlights and real results
**Best for:** Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, Pinterest

### Structure

```
CREATOR CONTEXT: [Who they are, what niche, approximate size -- NO fictional creators]

WHAT THEY DID: [Specific strategy or change they made]

RESULTS: [Observable outcome -- growth, engagement shift, earnings bracket -- labeled as community-reported if applicable]

LESSON: [What other creators can take away and apply]

CTA: [Platform-appropriate call to action]

NOTE: Only use verifiable creator stories. If the creator or data cannot be confirmed, mark [UNVERIFIED] and hold for verification before posting.
```

### Platform Versions

**Instagram (carousel):**
- Slide 1: "[Niche] creator did [thing] and [result]"
- Slides 2-3: What they did and why it worked
- Final slide: Lesson + CTA
- Caption: 125-150 chars
- Hashtags: 3-5, rotation set

**TikTok (video):**
- Storytelling format, 60+ seconds
- Start with the result to hook attention
- 3-5 hashtags

**Facebook:**
- 100-250 word narrative expanding on the story
- No hashtags or 1-2 max

**Pinterest:**
- Evergreen pin: "[Niche] Creator Strategy That Actually Works [Year]"
- SEO description, 2-5 hashtags

**Reddit:**
- Share only if directly relevant to a thread discussion
- Never fabricate results or invent creators

### Example Fill

```
CREATOR CONTEXT: Education creator, around 15K followers, posting study tips and exam strategies.

WHAT THEY DID: Switched from short-form clips under 60 seconds to 90-second mini-lessons. Focused on search-intent topics using Creator Search Insights.

RESULTS: Qualified view count increased noticeably. Their RPM sits in the higher range for the education niche (community-reported: education RPMs tend to run $1.00-$2.50).

LESSON: Longer content that answers specific questions earns more than short clips that go viral. Search intent + retention is the formula.

CTA: What's your niche? Drop it below and I'll tell you what content format tends to earn the most.

NOTE: [UNVERIFIED: specific RPM figures are community-reported ranges, not official TikTok data. Verify creator details before posting.]
```

## CT6: Myth Buster

**Pillar:** Common misconceptions about TikTok monetization
**Best for:** TikTok video, Instagram carousel, X thread, Facebook post

### Structure

```
MYTH: "[Common misconception stated confidently]"

ACTUALLY: [The real answer, stated plainly with specifics]

PROOF: [Data point, official source, or observable evidence]

CTA: [Platform-appropriate call to action]
```

### Platform Versions

**TikTok:**
- Open with the myth stated seriously, then correct it
- Text on screen: MYTH / FACT labels
- Caption: First 100 chars = the myth (hooks curiosity)
- 60+ seconds, 3-5 hashtags

**Instagram (carousel):**
- Slide 1: "MYTH:" in bold with the misconception
- Slide 2: "ACTUALLY:" with the correction
- Slide 3: Proof point with source
- Slide 4: CTA
- Caption: 125-150 chars with keyword
- Hashtags: 3-5, rotation set

**X:**
- Single tweet: "Myth: [X]. Reality: [Y]. [Proof point]."
- 1-2 hashtags

**Facebook:**
- 100-250 words expanding on the correction
- Include the source for the proof point

**Reddit:**
- Comment format only: answer someone's question with the correction
- Include proof point, no TCP mention

### Example Fill

```
MYTH: "You need 10,000 followers to make money on TikTok."

ACTUALLY: The Creator Rewards Program requires 10,000 followers to join. But follower count after that point has zero effect on how much you earn. A 12K account and a 500K account with the same views and RPM make the same money.

PROOF: TikTok's Creator Rewards FAQ states payouts are based on qualified views, not follower count.

CTA: Stop chasing followers. Start optimizing your RPM.
```

## CT7: Trending Sound/Format

**Pillar:** Current trends and how to use them
**Best for:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, X

### Structure

```
TREND: [Sound name, format type, or trending template]

WHY IT WORKS: [1-2 sentences on why the algorithm is pushing this or why audiences respond]

HOW TO USE IT: [Specific adaptation advice for TikTok creators in different niches]

SHELF LIFE: [How long this trend is likely to be relevant -- days, weeks, or evergreen]

CTA: [Platform-appropriate call to action]
```

### Platform Versions

**TikTok (video):**
- Use the trending sound/format directly in the video
- Caption: Name the trend + your niche spin in first 100 chars
- 60+ seconds, 3-5 hashtags

**Instagram Reels:**
- Adapt the TikTok version for Reels
- Caption: 125-150 chars, keyword-rich
- Hashtags: 3-5, rotation set

**X:**
- Tweet: "[Trend] is blowing up on TikTok. Here's how [niche] creators are using it."
- 1-2 hashtags

**Pinterest:**
- Not ideal for trending content (Pinterest favors evergreen). Skip unless the trend has lasting search value.

**Facebook:**
- Short text post flagging the trend + link to example if available
- 1-2 hashtags max

### Example Fill

```
TREND: The "day in my life as a [job title]" format with a specific daily earnings breakdown overlay.

WHY IT WORKS: Combines curiosity (the job), voyeurism (day-in-life), and concrete numbers (earnings). Viewers watch to the end because the earnings reveal is the payoff. High completion rate feeds the algorithm.

HOW TO USE IT: For any monetization niche, show what a real work day looks like and overlay your approximate earnings from each activity. Be honest about the range. Viewers can spot inflated numbers and will call it out in comments.

SHELF LIFE: This format has been cycling for months and keeps resurfacing. Probably 2-4 more weeks at peak, then settles into an evergreen format people return to.

CTA: What's your niche? I'll tell you how to spin this for your content.
```
